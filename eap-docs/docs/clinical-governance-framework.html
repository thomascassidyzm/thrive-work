1. Executive Summary

Purpose Statement:

This Clinical Governance Framework establishes the comprehensive system through which Thrive ensures content quality, user safety, and appropriate clinical escalation. It defines the processes, responsibilities, and standards that govern the development, delivery, and continuous improvement of the Thrive platform.

Framework Objectives:

  • Ensure all content is clinically sound, evidence-based, and appropriate for the target user population
  • Protect user safety through clear scope definition, contraindication identification, and robust crisis response protocols
  • Establish clear pathways for clinical escalation and referral to appropriate services
  • Maintain regulatory compliance and professional standards
  • Drive continuous quality improvement through systematic monitoring and review

Governance Approach:

Thrive operates as a wellness and resilience platform under clinical oversight, integrating evidence-based content with clear safety protocols and defined escalation pathways to CQC-regulated clinical services when needed. This framework ensures a responsible, safe, and effective service within appropriate boundaries.

Key Stakeholder Accountability:

  • Clinical Director: Overall clinical governance accountability and framework oversight
  • Content Team: Development and maintenance of evidence-based platform content
  • Support Team: User safety monitoring and escalation protocol implementation
  • Quality & Compliance: Monitoring, audit, and regulatory compliance
  • Executive Leadership: Strategic oversight and resource allocation

Thrive is contraindicated or requires additional clinical support for individuals experiencing:

  • Severe mental health conditions requiring medical intervention - Including but not limited to: severe depression, psychosis, acute mania, severe PTSD
  • Active psychosis or delusional thinking - Users presenting with symptoms of psychosis are referred to emergency services
  • Acute substance intoxication or withdrawal - Immediate medical assessment required
  • Safeguarding concerns - Risk to children or vulnerable adults triggers safeguarding protocols
  • Imminent risk to self or others - Emergency escalation protocols activated
  • [INSERT ADDITIONAL CONTRAINDICATIONS AS RELEVANT]

Contraindication Screening:

Users are screened for contraindications at:

  • Initial platform registration/onboarding
  • Live chat interactions (trained team identifies warning signs)

  • Behavioral indicators identified through platform use patterns (if applicable)

3.4 Crisis Identification & Response

Signs of Crisis:

Platform users and live chat support are trained to recognize indicators of crisis, including:

  • Explicit statements of suicidal ideation or intent
  • Discussion of self-harm plans or means
  • Expressions of hopelessness, worthlessness, or being a burden to others
  • Psychotic symptoms or delusional thinking
  • Acute severe distress or panic
  • Disclosure of immediate risk to self or others
  • Safeguarding concerns (child protection, vulnerable adult abuse)

Immediate Response Protocol:

  1. Acknowledge & Validate
    • Respond with empathy and without judgment
    • Acknowledge the person's distress
    • Avoid dismissing or minimizing concerns
  2. Assess Immediate Risk
    • If live chat: ask directly about suicidal thoughts, plans, and means
    • Determine immediacy and severity of risk
    • Document all relevant information
  3. Provide Emergency Contacts
    • Display emergency contact information prominently
    • Encourage immediate contact with appropriate service
    • Do not delay emergency response while gathering information
  4. Escalate Internally
    • Alert supervisor/clinical lead immediately
    • Document incident in incident reporting system
    • Follow escalation pathway appropriate to risk level
  5. Follow Safeguarding Protocols
    • If safeguarding concern identified, follow organizational safeguarding policy
    • Report to relevant authorities where required by law

Emergency Contacts to Display:

  • Emergency Services: 999 (UK) - for immediate risk to life
  • Samaritans: 116 123 (24/7 free helpline)
  • Shout Crisis Text Line: Text SHOUT to 85258 (24/7 free text support)
  • NHS 111: 111 - for urgent but non-life-threatening concerns
  • Local Crisis Team: [INSERT LOCAL CONTACT IF APPLICABLE]
  • GP/Out of Hours GP: Encourage user to contact their registered GP

Escalation to Emergency Services:

If imminent risk to life is identified, users are directed to call 999 immediately. Live chat support staff do not attempt crisis intervention beyond signposting and support-seeking encouragement. Emergency services are the appropriate response for active crisis.

4. Escalation & Referral Pathways

4.1 Escalation Triggers

When to Escalate:

Escalation pathways are activated when users present with needs beyond the scope of the Thrive platform. Triggers include:

  • Clinical Need: Symptoms suggesting moderate-to-severe mental health condition requiring professional assessment (e.g., persistent low mood impacting function, significant anxiety, trauma symptoms)
  • User Request: User explicitly requests referral to clinical services or professional support
  • Lack of Progress: User engaging with platform but reporting no improvement or worsening symptoms over [TIME PERIOD - e.g., 4-6 weeks]
  • Safety Concern: Any indication of risk to self or others, though not immediate crisis
  • Contraindication Identified: User screening reveals contraindication to self-directed wellness platform
  • Complex Presentation: Multiple co-occurring difficulties requiring coordinated professional intervention

Automated vs Manual Triggers:

  • Automated triggers: [DESCRIBE - e.g., "Assessment tool scores indicating clinical threshold, keyword detection in live chat for crisis terms"]
  • Manual triggers: [DESCRIBE - e.g., "Live chat support staff clinical judgment, user self-referral requests, clinical oversight team review"]

Staff Training on Recognition:

  • All live chat support staff trained in escalation trigger recognition
  • Regular refresher training on risk assessment and escalation protocols (minimum [FREQUENCY - e.g., annually])
  • Clinical supervision and case discussion for complex scenarios
  • Access to clinical lead for real-time consultation when needed

B. Emergency Services

Criteria: Imminent risk to self or others, acute severe mental health crisis requiring immediate intervention.

Process:

  1. Immediately direct user to call 999 (or local emergency number)
  2. Provide emergency contact information prominently and clearly
  3. If live chat, encourage user to seek immediate in-person support (family member, friend, neighbor) while emergency services are contacted
  4. Do not delay emergency contact for information gathering or assessment
  5. Document incident immediately in incident reporting system
  6. Alert clinical lead and follow internal escalation protocols

Documentation Requirements:

  • Time and date of interaction
  • Summary of user statements and presenting concerns
  • Emergency contacts provided to user
  • Actions taken (staff response, internal escalation)
  • Follow-up actions planned (if user continues engagement with platform)

6. Incident Reporting & Management

Definition of Reportable Incident:

An incident is any event involving the Thrive platform or services that:

  • Results in or has potential to result in harm to a user
  • Involves safeguarding concerns (child protection, vulnerable adult abuse)
  • Represents a significant deviation from standard protocols
  • Involves user complaint about safety or clinical concerns
  • Includes crisis presentation or emergency service involvement
  • Involves data breach or confidentiality compromise
  • Represents a near-miss that could have resulted in harm
  • Reveals a gap in service provision or protocol

Incident Reporting Process:

  1. Immediate Action (within 24 hours):
    • Take any necessary immediate action to safeguard user or others
    • Alert clinical lead/supervisor of incident
    • Complete initial incident report form (see Appendix C) with all available information
    • Submit incident report to [RECIPIENT - e.g., Clinical Director and Governance Committee]
  2. Incident Classification (within 48 hours):
    • Clinical Director reviews incident and classifies severity: Low, Moderate, Serious, Major
    • Determines level of investigation required
    • Identifies if external reporting is required (safeguarding, CQC, information commissioner, etc.)
  3. Investigation (timeline based on severity):
    • Assign investigation lead
    • Gather all relevant information (records, staff accounts, user communications, etc.)
    • Interview relevant staff members
    • Review against protocols and standards
    • Complete investigation report with findings
  4. Root Cause Analysis:
    • Identify contributing factors (individual, system, process, training, etc.)
    • Distinguish between root causes and symptoms
    • Consider human factors and organizational context
    • Apply structured analysis framework (e.g., 5 Whys, Fishbone diagram)
  5. Corrective Actions:
    • Develop action plan to address root causes
    • Assign responsibility and timelines for each action
    • Actions may include: protocol updates, staff training, system changes, policy revisions, etc.
    • Document all corrective actions in incident report
  6. Closure & Learning:
    • Track implementation of corrective actions
    • Review effectiveness after implementation
    • Share learning across team (anonymized case studies, team meetings)
    • Update policies, procedures, and training materials as needed
    • Close incident with final report to Governance Committee

Investigation Procedures:

Incident Severity Investigation Timeline Investigation Lead Reporting
Low Complete within 2 weeks Team Lead Summary to Clinical Director
Moderate Complete within 1 week Clinical Director Full report to Governance Committee
Serious Complete within 72 hours Clinical Director + External Advisor if needed Full report to Board and relevant authorities
Major Immediate investigation External Independent Investigation Full report to Board, authorities, regulators

Corrective Action Tracking:

  • All corrective actions logged in incident management system with responsible person and deadline
  • Monthly review of open corrective actions at governance meetings
  • Completed actions verified before closure
  • Impact assessment conducted post-implementation

Reporting to Relevant Authorities:

Certain incidents require mandatory reporting to external bodies:

  • Safeguarding Concerns: Report to Local Authority Safeguarding Team within 24 hours
  • Data Breaches: Report to Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours if required by GDPR
  • Serious Incidents: Report to relevant regulators or oversight bodies as applicable
  • Criminal Activity: Report to police where appropriate

Response Timeline Commitments:

Stage Timeline Action
Acknowledgment Within 2 working days Confirm receipt of complaint, provide reference number, outline process and timeline
Initial Assessment Within 5 working days Review complaint, gather relevant information, determine investigation needs
Investigation Within 15 working days Complete investigation (may be extended for complex cases with notification to complainant)

Investigation Process:

  2. Information Gathering: Collect all relevant documentation, records, staff accounts, user communications
  3. Analysis: Review complaint against service standards, policies, and regulatory requirements
  4. Staff Consultation: Speak with relevant staff members involved (supportive approach, learning-focused)
  5. Findings: Determine whether complaint is upheld (fully or partially) or not upheld, with clear rationale
  6. Response Preparation: Draft response including findings, apology if appropriate, actions taken, and learning identified
  7. Clinical Director Review: All responses reviewed and signed off by Clinical Director before sending
  8. Response Sent: Send to complainant with clear explanation and information about further escalation options if not satisfied

Resolution & Learning:

  • All complaints reviewed for learning opportunities and service improvement
  • Trends and patterns analyzed quarterly at Governance Committee meetings
  • Action plans developed to address systemic issues identified through complaints
  • Staff feedback and support provided where complaints involve individual performance
  • Policies and procedures updated in response to justified complaints
  • Complaints data included in annual governance review

External Escalation Options:

If users are not satisfied with the internal complaints process outcome, they are informed of external options:

  • Ombudsman Services: [INSERT RELEVANT OMBUDSMAN - e.g., "Independent Healthcare Sector Complaints Adjudication Service (ISCAS)" or similar]
  • Professional Bodies: If complaint relates to conduct of regulated professional (e.g., HCPC, GMC, NMC)

Staff are supported through the complaints process and complaints data is used constructively for service development.

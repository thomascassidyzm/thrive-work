1. Introduction

Document Purpose:

This Charter establishes the ClearMinds B2B Governance Board as the primary oversight body for ClearMinds B2B Ltd, a special purpose vehicle created to deliver workplace wellbeing services to business and insurance partners.

Why a Separate Governance Board?

ClearMinds B2B operates in a distinct regulatory and commercial environment from B2C services:

  • Higher governance requirements: B2B partners (insurance companies, large employers) require institutional-grade assurance and oversight
  • Different clinical oversight needs: Integration with CQC-regulated clinical pathways (Smart Start Minds) requires dedicated clinical governance
  • Contractual obligations: B2B partnerships involve formal SLAs, quality commitments, and regulatory compliance not present in B2C
  • Risk profile: Occupational health and workplace wellbeing involves different stakeholder accountability than consumer wellness

2.3 Rationale for SPV Structure

Strategic Reasons for Separate Entity:

  1. Governance Requirements: B2B partnerships require higher level of clinical and commercial governance than B2C consumer platform
  2. Clinical Pathway Integration: Formal relationship with Dr. Thomas Dannhauser and Smart Start Minds (CQC-regulated) requires dedicated oversight and medical additionality
  3. Regulatory Clarity: Clear separation of B2C wellness product from B2B workplace health offering
  4. Partnership Confidence: Insurance partners prefer contracting with entity that has dedicated governance structure
  5. Risk Management: Separate entity provides liability isolation between B2C and B2B operations
  6. Future Flexibility: Allows for potential merger into future Thrive brand or independent evolution

3. Board Purpose & Remit

3.1 Primary Purpose

The ClearMinds B2B Governance Board exists to:

  • Provide strategic oversight and direction for ClearMinds B2B operations
  • Ensure clinical governance and user safety across all B2B partnerships
  • Maintain regulatory compliance and professional standards
  • Oversee quality assurance and continuous improvement
  • Monitor partnership performance and risk management
  • Ensure accountability to partners, users, and regulators

3.2 Scope of Authority

The Board has authority over:

Area Board Authority Limitations/Escalations
Clinical Governance Full authority to approve clinical governance frameworks, safety protocols, escalation pathways Must align with professional body standards
Quality Standards Set and monitor quality standards for B2B services Cannot compromise below regulatory minimums
Partnership Approval Approve new B2B partnerships (AXA, Towergate, etc.) Financial implications above [THRESHOLD] require parent board approval
Incident Management Oversee serious incident investigations and responses Must report Category 1 incidents to parent board
Operational Budget Approve operational budget within agreed parameters Budget ceiling set by [PARENT ENTITY/SHAREHOLDERS]
Staff/Clinical Team Oversee recruitment and performance of clinical oversight team Within budget constraints

3.3 What the Board Does NOT Cover

The following remain outside the Board's remit:

  • B2C consumer product governance (separate governance if applicable)
  • Core platform technology development (managed by [TECHNOLOGY TEAM/PARENT])
  • Group financial strategy and investment decisions
  • Thrive brand strategy and future product roadmap

5. Responsibilities & Duties

5.1 Clinical Governance Responsibilities

The Board must:

  • Approve and annually review Clinical Governance Framework
  • Review and approve user safety protocols, disclaimers, and contraindications
  • Oversee escalation pathways (to SSM, emergency services, GP)
  • Review serious incidents involving user safety
  • Monitor quality metrics (user safety, clinical appropriateness, escalation effectiveness)
  • Ensure clinical team adequately qualified, trained, and supervised
  • Approve content creation and review processes
  • Ensure compliance with professional standards (BPS, HCPC, etc.)

5.2 Partnership Oversight Responsibilities

The Board must:

  • Approve new B2B partnerships (insurance, corporate)
  • Review partnership performance quarterly (SLA compliance, quality metrics)
  • Oversee Smart Start Minds partnership and clinical pathway
  • Ensure partnership contracts protect users and meet quality standards
  • Review partner feedback and complaints
  • Approve changes to service model that affect partners

5.3 Regulatory Compliance Responsibilities

The Board must:

  • Ensure GDPR compliance and data protection standards
  • Ensure ASA advertising compliance (claims, disclaimers)
  • Monitor regulatory changes affecting workplace wellbeing services
  • Ensure professional indemnity insurance is adequate and current
  • Oversee compliance with employment law (if applicable to service users)
  • Ensure complaints procedures meet regulatory requirements

5.4 Quality Assurance & Risk Management

The Board must:

  • Review quality metrics quarterly (engagement, outcomes, satisfaction)
  • Oversee continuous quality improvement initiatives
  • Maintain and review risk register
  • Review incident reports and implement learning
  • Ensure business continuity and disaster recovery plans exist and are tested
  • Approve annual quality audit schedule

5.5 Strategic Oversight

The Board must:

  • Approve annual strategic plan for ClearMinds B2B
  • Review financial performance against budget
  • Approve operational budget
  • Consider market opportunities and growth strategy
  • Align with parent company/Thrive roadmap where relevant

6. Meetings & Procedures

6.1 Meeting Frequency

Meeting Type Frequency Purpose
Full Board Meeting Quarterly (minimum 4 per year) Strategic oversight, quality review, governance approval
Clinical Governance Subcommittee Bi-monthly (6 per year) Detailed clinical governance, incident review, safety monitoring
Extraordinary Meeting As needed Serious incidents, urgent decisions, crisis response
Annual Strategy Meeting Annually Annual planning, budget approval, strategic review

6.2 Meeting Logistics

  • Notice Period: Minimum 14 days for regular meetings, 48 hours for extraordinary meetings
  • Agenda: Circulated 7 days before meeting, members may request additions up to 3 days before
  • Papers: Supporting papers circulated 5 days before meeting
  • Format: In-person preferred, video conference acceptable, hybrid model supported
  • Duration: Typically 2-3 hours, maximum 4 hours
  • Secretary: [ROLE - e.g., Governance Manager] responsible for logistics and minutes

6.3 Standard Agenda Items

Every regular Board meeting must include:

  1. Apologies and quorum confirmation
  2. Declarations of interest
  3. Minutes of previous meeting and matters arising
  4. Clinical governance report (from Clinical Director)
  5. Partnership performance report
  6. Quality metrics review
  7. Incident report (if any serious incidents since last meeting)
  8. Complaints report
  9. Regulatory update (compliance, legal changes)
  10. Financial report (budget vs. actual)
  11. Risk register review
  12. AOB and date of next meeting

6.4 Minutes & Records

  • Minutes Preparation: Draft circulated within 7 days of meeting
  • Approval: Approved at next meeting, then signed by Chair
  • Distribution: Approved minutes to all Board members, parent board (summary), available to regulators/auditors
  • Retention: Permanent record, securely stored
  • Confidentiality: Minutes confidential unless designated sections approved for external sharing

7. Decision Making

7.1 Decision-Making Principles

The Board operates on principles of:

  • User Safety First: Decisions prioritize user safety over commercial considerations
  • Evidence-Based: Decisions grounded in data, evidence, and expert input
  • Transparency: Decision rationale clearly documented
  • Collaboration: Consensus sought where possible
  • Timely: Decisions made without undue delay, especially on safety matters

7.2 Voting Procedures

Decision Type Voting Threshold Notes
Routine Operational Matters Simple majority Standard approvals, routine governance
Clinical Governance Changes 2/3 majority Clinical Director must support
New Partnership Approval 2/3 majority Due diligence required before vote
Charter Amendments 2/3 majority Parent board approval may be required
Emergency Safety Decisions Chair + Clinical Director authority Ratified at next Board meeting

7.3 Conflicts of Interest

Declaration:

  • All Board members must declare potential conflicts at start of each meeting
  • Annual declaration of interests form completed
  • Register of interests maintained by Board Secretary

Management:

  • Member with conflict must declare nature and extent
  • Board decides if conflict requires member to: (a) abstain from vote, (b) leave room during discussion, or (c) no action needed if minimal conflict
  • Decision and rationale recorded in minutes

Standing Conflicts:

Dr. Thomas Dannhauser (SSM Liaison) has standing conflict on decisions directly affecting SSM partnership terms - will abstain from commercial negotiations but participate in quality/clinical oversight discussions.

8. Reporting & Accountability

8.1 Reporting Lines

The Board reports to:

Stakeholder Reporting Frequency Content
Parent Company Board Quarterly Board meeting summary, financial performance, key decisions, risk register
Insurance Partners (AXA, etc.) Quarterly Quality metrics, SLA performance, partnership review, incident summary
Shareholders (if applicable) Annually Annual governance report, financial statements, strategy
Regulators As required Incident notifications, compliance reports, audit responses

8.2 Annual Governance Report

The Board must produce an Annual Governance Report covering:

  • Board composition, attendance, and any changes
  • Summary of key decisions made
  • Clinical governance performance (safety metrics, incidents, learning)
  • Partnership performance (quality, satisfaction, SLA compliance)
  • Regulatory compliance status
  • Quality improvement initiatives
  • Risk management and major risks addressed
  • Financial summary
  • Forward look and strategic priorities for next year

8.3 Accountability Framework

Board is accountable for:

  • User safety and quality of service
  • Partnership performance and partner satisfaction
  • Regulatory compliance
  • Financial stewardship within budget
  • Transparent, ethical governance

Board is NOT accountable for:

  • Day-to-day operational delivery (management responsibility)
  • Individual clinical decisions (clinician responsibility)
  • Platform technology development (technology team responsibility)
  • Group strategy (parent board responsibility)

9. Subcommittees

9.1 Clinical Governance Subcommittee

Purpose: Deep-dive clinical governance oversight, incident review, safety monitoring

Composition:

  • Clinical Director (Chair)
  • SSM Liaison (Dr. Thomas Dannhauser or nominee)
  • ClearMinds Clinical Lead (if separate from Clinical Director)
  • Legal/Compliance Representative
  • Co-opted clinical experts as needed

Meetings: Bi-monthly (6 per year)

Responsibilities:

  • Detailed review of clinical governance framework implementation
  • Review all serious incidents in detail
  • Monitor safety metrics and trends
  • Approve content from clinical perspective
  • Ensure escalation pathways functioning effectively
  • Recommend clinical governance improvements to Board

Reporting: Reports to full Board at each quarterly meeting

9.2 Optional Additional Subcommittees

The Board may establish additional subcommittees as needed:

  • Audit & Risk Committee: Financial oversight, risk management, compliance audit
  • Partnerships Committee: Partnership development, contract review, partner relationship management
  • Remuneration Committee: (If Board grows larger and senior appointments made)

10. Key Relationships

10.1 Relationship with Smart Start Minds

Nature of Relationship:

  • Strategic clinical partnership (CQC-regulated pathway)
  • SSM represented on Board through Liaison role (Dr. Thomas Dannhauser)
  • Formal partnership agreement in place
  • Joint quality oversight through partnership meetings (separate from Board)

Board's Role:

  • Approve partnership agreement and any material changes
  • Monitor partnership quality and performance
  • Review user outcomes from SSM referrals
  • Ensure data sharing and governance arrangements appropriate
  • Address any partnership issues or concerns

10.2 Relationship with Insurance Partners (AXA, Towergate, etc.)

Nature of Relationship:

  • Contractual B2B partnerships
  • Partners are customers and stakeholders
  • Potential for partner observer on Board (non-voting)

Board's Role:

  • Approve partnership contracts
  • Monitor SLA compliance and quality commitments
  • Review partner feedback and address concerns
  • Ensure service meets partner requirements
  • Maintain partner confidence through transparent governance

10.3 Relationship with Management Team

Boundary: Board sets strategy and policy; management executes operationally

Management Accountability to Board:

  • Deliver quarterly performance reports
  • Implement Board decisions
  • Escalate serious issues promptly
  • Provide information and analysis for Board decision-making
  • Maintain day-to-day governance and quality standards

Board Support to Management:

  • Provide strategic direction and clarity
  • Approve resources and budgets
  • Support management in partnership negotiations
  • Provide clinical and governance expertise

11. Charter Review

Review Schedule:

  • Annual Review: Charter reviewed at Annual Strategy Meeting each year
  • Trigger Reviews: Charter reviewed sooner if:
    • Significant regulatory changes
    • Major partnership developments
    • Organizational restructure
    • Serious governance failure
    • 2/3 of Board requests review

Amendment Process:

  1. Proposed amendments circulated to all Board members 14 days before vote
  2. Discussion and amendment at Board meeting
  3. 2/3 majority vote required to approve changes
  4. Parent company board approval required for material changes
  5. Amended charter circulated to all stakeholders

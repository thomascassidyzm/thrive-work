4-Week Implementation Guide & Checklist

1. Executive Summary

This guide provides a comprehensive 4-week implementation plan to prepare your organization for the partner meeting with AXA, Towergate, and other insurance partners. The plan is designed to be systematic, actionable, and trackable, ensuring all critical documents and information are ready on time.

PARTNER REQUIREMENTS (Priority 1)

Insurance partners (AXA, Towergate) have specifically requested:

  1. Clinical Governance Framework - How we ensure content quality and user safety
  2. Medical Compliance Statement - Our regulatory positioning and evidence base
  3. Smart Start Minds Partnership - CQC-regulated clinical pathway integration

These three documents are ESSENTIAL for the partner meeting.

This implementation guide provides:

  • Phased Approach: Documents are prioritized into three tiers (Priority 1, 2, 3) based on urgency and importance
  • Clear Ownership: Each document has assigned owners, due dates, and status tracking
  • Progress Tracking: Comprehensive checklists and status indicators throughout
  • Information Gathering: Detailed tables identifying what information is needed, from whom, and why
  • Meeting Preparation: Step-by-step guidance for preparing for the partner meeting
  • Timeline Management: Week-by-week breakdown of activities and deliverables

The guide is designed to be used as a living document - update it daily as you make progress, gather information, and complete tasks. By following this plan systematically, you will be fully prepared for a successful partner meeting.

6. Meeting Preparation Checklist

1 WEEK BEFORE MEETING:

  • All Priority 1 documents complete (A, B, C)
  • Internal review completed by relevant stakeholders
  • Sign-offs obtained (clinical director, legal, leadership)
  • Documents formatted and polished professionally
  • PDF versions created for distribution
  • Executive summary or cover letter prepared

3-5 DAYS BEFORE MEETING:

  • Documents sent to partners for pre-read (if appropriate)
  • Presentation deck prepared (if needed for meeting)
  • Q&A preparation session held with internal team
  • Key stakeholders briefed on meeting objectives and content
  • SSM contact alerted (in case partners have questions about pathway)
  • Meeting agenda confirmed with partners

1-2 DAYS BEFORE MEETING:

  • Final document review (check for any errors or placeholders)
  • Print copies prepared (if in-person meeting)
  • Digital copies accessible (if virtual meeting - Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.)
  • Meeting logistics confirmed (location, Zoom link, dial-in, etc.)
  • Note-taker assigned for meeting
  • Follow-up action tracker template ready
  • Demo environment ready and tested (if applicable)

MEETING DAY:

  • All documents accessible and organized
  • Key team members present/available (clinical, operations, tech)
  • Demo environment ready (if showing the platform)
  • Questions and concerns log ready to capture partner feedback
  • Follow-up plan ready to discuss at end of meeting
  • Business cards / contact info ready to share

7. Critical Success Factors

MUST-HAVES FOR PARTNER MEETING

  1. ✅ Clinical Governance Framework - Complete and signed off by clinical director
  2. ✅ Medical Compliance Statement - Legally reviewed and approved
  3. ✅ SSM Partnership Document - With verified CQC details and SSM approval
  4. ✅ Evidence Base - Research citations and outcome data ready to share
  5. ✅ Q&A Preparation - Team briefed on likely questions and answers aligned

Without these 5 elements, strongly recommend postponing the meeting.

QUALITY STANDARDS:

Before the meeting, ensure all documents meet these quality standards:

  • All documents professionally formatted with consistent styling
  • No [PLACEHOLDER] text remaining - all sections filled in with actual information
  • All cross-references verified and working (e.g., "see Section 3.2")
  • Legal review completed on compliance-sensitive documents (especially Document B)
  • SSM partnership details verified directly with Smart Start Minds
  • Internal stakeholder sign-off obtained from all relevant parties
  • Documents accessible in multiple formats (PDF for distribution, print for in-person)
  • All contact information and credentials up-to-date and accurate
  • Grammar, spelling, and formatting checked thoroughly
  • Version control in place (document version numbers, dates, owners)

TEAM ALIGNMENT:

  • All team members understand ClearMinds's positioning (wellness, not medical device)
  • Messaging consistent across all documents and team members
  • Roles clear for meeting (who presents what, who handles Q&A on specific topics)
  • Escalation plan in place if difficult questions arise
  • Follow-up process agreed upon (who sends what, by when)

1. Executive Summary

Decision Context:

We need to establish the optimal legal and operational structure for delivering workplace wellbeing services to B2B partners (insurance companies, corporate clients). This decision affects governance, partnerships, liability, and future flexibility.

Key Findings:

  • ✅ Single company with Corporate division is recommended for ClearMinds Corporate
  • B2B requires higher governance standards than B2C justifying structural separation
  • Formal SSM partnership requires dedicated governance and medical oversight
  • Insurance partners prefer contracting with entities that have clear, dedicated governance
  • Structure allows future flexibility for Thrive brand evolution

Recommended Structure:

ClearMinds Ltd - Private limited company (single registered company) with dedicated Governance Board, using ClearMinds platform technology under license/internal arrangement.

2. Strategic Context

Entity/Brand Current Status Target Market Governance Level
ClearMinds Consumer Live consumer wellness platform Individual consumers Standard consumer product governance
ClearMinds Corporate Being established Insurance partners, corporate clients Institutional-grade governance required
Thrive Future brand (not yet live) Consumer wellness (ambitious vision) To be determined

2.2 Strategic Drivers

Why B2B Matters Now:

  • AXA Partnership Opportunity: Immediate B2B opportunity with major insurance partner
  • Towergate Interest: Additional partnership discussions underway
  • Revenue Potential: B2B partnerships offer more predictable, higher-value contracts than B2C
  • Market Positioning: Workplace wellbeing is growing market segment
  • Clinical Credibility: SSM partnership strengthens offering for institutional buyers

Why Not Wait for Thrive:

  • Thrive brand/product not ready for market (still in development)
  • AXA opportunity is NOW - can't delay for future brand launch
  • B2B partnerships have different requirements than consumer product
  • ClearMinds platform exists and works - use what we have
  • Can migrate to Thrive umbrella later if strategic

2.3 Key Tension

The Core Challenge:

ClearMinds B2C doesn't need intensive clinical governance or medical oversight - it's a consumer wellness app. But ClearMinds Corporate partners (insurance, corporate) REQUIRE institutional-grade governance, clinical pathways to CQC-regulated services, and dedicated oversight. This fundamental difference drives the need for separate entity structure.

3. Requirements for B2B Entity

3.1 Governance Requirements

B2B entity must provide:

Requirement Why Essential B2C Comparison
Dedicated Governance Board Insurance partners require clear accountability and oversight B2C: Product governance sufficient
Clinical Director Oversight Professional clinical governance for workplace health offering B2C: Content quality checks sufficient
Formal SSM Partnership CQC-regulated escalation pathway required for occupational health B2C: Optional signposting to resources
Regular Board Meetings Quarterly governance review, partnership oversight B2C: As-needed product reviews
Quality Metrics & SLAs Contractual commitments to partners B2C: User satisfaction tracking
Incident Reporting to Board Serious incidents affecting partner employees escalated formally B2C: Standard customer service

3.2 Partnership Requirements

B2B partnerships require entity to:

  • Contract as Principal: Clear legal entity for partnership agreements
  • Professional Indemnity Insurance: Appropriate coverage for B2B services
  • Formal Governance Documentation: Clinical Governance Framework, Medical Compliance Statement, etc.
  • SLA Commitments: Uptime, response times, quality metrics
  • Regulatory Compliance: GDPR, data protection, advertising standards, professional standards
  • Quality Assurance: Regular audits, metrics, continuous improvement
  • Transparent Governance: Board minutes, governance reports available to partners

3.3 Clinical Pathway Requirements

Integration with Smart Start Minds requires:

  • Formal Partnership Agreement: With Dr. Thomas Dannhauser and SSM
  • Clinical Oversight: Medical Director or Clinical Director on Board
  • Escalation Protocols: Clear pathways for users needing clinical support
  • Data Sharing Agreements: GDPR-compliant sharing with SSM
  • Quality Monitoring: Regular review of referral appropriateness and outcomes
  • Joint Governance: SSM representation on Board or regular partnership meetings

4. Entity Structure Options

Option A: Trading Name (No Separate Entity)

Aspect Description
Structure "ClearMinds Corporate" is just a brand/trading name; contracts signed by existing ClearMinds Ltd
Governance B2B governance subcommittee within existing structure
Cost Low (no company formation, minimal admin)
Speed Fast (immediate)

Pros:

  • ✅ Simple and fast to implement
  • ✅ Lower administrative burden
  • ✅ Shared resources with B2C
  • ✅ No company formation costs

Cons:

  • ❌ Insufficient governance separation for insurance partners
  • ❌ B2C and B2B governance mixed (different requirements)
  • ❌ Liability not isolated (B2C issues affect B2B contracts)
  • ❌ Less attractive to institutional buyers who prefer dedicated entities
  • ❌ SSM partnership lacks clear governance home
  • ❌ Harder to justify dedicated Clinical Director role

Verdict: NOT RECOMMENDED - Insufficient for institutional B2B requirements

Option B: Division/Business Unit (Internal Separation)

Aspect Description
Structure ClearMinds Ltd has "B2B Division" with separate P&L, management, governance structure
Governance Dedicated B2B Board (division board) reporting to main company board
Cost Low-Medium (internal restructure, governance setup)
Speed Medium (2-4 weeks to establish)

Pros:

  • ✅ Clear governance separation without legal entity cost
  • ✅ Can have dedicated B2B Board with Clinical Director
  • ✅ Shared platform and infrastructure (efficiency)
  • ✅ Simpler tax and accounting than separate entity
  • ✅ Easier resource sharing between B2C and B2B

Cons:

  • ⚠️ Partners may prefer separate legal entity
  • ⚠️ Liability still shared with B2C operations
  • ⚠️ Less flexibility for future sale or spin-off
  • ⚠️ SSM partnership bound to main entity
  • ⚠️ Professional indemnity insurance complexity

Verdict: RECOMMENDED - Optimal balance of speed, cost, and governance for ClearMinds Corporate

Option C: Wholly-Owned Subsidiary

Aspect Description
Structure ClearMinds Ltd - separate private limited company, 100% owned by ClearMinds Ltd
Governance Independent Governance Board with Clinical Director, reports to parent board strategically
Cost Medium (company formation ~£100, annual accounts filing, board costs)
Speed Medium (1-2 weeks company formation, 4-6 weeks governance setup)

Pros:

  • ✅ Clear legal entity for partnership contracts
  • ✅ Dedicated governance appropriate for institutional buyers
  • ✅ Liability isolation between B2C and B2B
  • ✅ SSM partnership clarity - clear entity for formal agreement
  • ✅ Professional credibility with insurance partners
  • ✅ Clean financial tracking for B2B performance
  • ✅ Future flexibility - can merge into Thrive or remain independent
  • ✅ Clinical governance home - natural entity for Clinical Director role
  • ✅ Insurance clarity - separate professional indemnity policy
  • ✅ Dr. Thomas Dannhauser relationship - formal partnership with dedicated entity

Cons:

  • ⚠️ Higher administrative burden (separate accounts, filings)
  • ⚠️ Company formation time and cost
  • ⚠️ More complex governance structure
  • ⚠️ Potential tax considerations (transfer pricing if significant)
  • ⚠️ Resource allocation between entities

Verdict: ACCEPTABLE - Analyzed but not chosen due to time/cost considerations

Option D: Independent Entity (Not Parent-Owned)

Aspect Description
Structure ClearMinds Ltd - completely independent company (not owned by ClearMinds Ltd)
Governance Fully independent Board, shareholders
Cost High (full company setup, separate operations)
Speed Slow (6-12 weeks full setup)

Pros:

  • ✅ Complete independence and flexibility
  • ✅ Could raise independent investment
  • ✅ No parent company constraints

Cons:

  • ❌ Platform license complexity (license from ClearMinds Ltd)
  • ❌ Loss of shared resources and efficiencies
  • ❌ Higher operational costs
  • ❌ Slower to market
  • ❌ Strategic misalignment risk

Verdict: NOT RECOMMENDED - Unnecessary complexity and cost

5. Recommendation: Single Company with Corporate Division (Option B)

Recommended Structure

Entity: ClearMinds Ltd (existing registered company)

Type: Private Limited Company with Corporate division

Governance: Single Board governing both B2C consumer product and Corporate workplace wellbeing

Platform: Shared ClearMinds platform serving both markets

Brand: Market-facing B2B brand as "ClearMinds Corporate"

Why This Structure Wins

Requirement How Single Company Meets It
Speed to Market No company formation delay - ClearMinds Ltd already exists and registered
Operational Simplicity Single legal entity, one set of accounts, unified operations
Clinical Governance Governance Board provides enhanced oversight benefiting both B2C and Corporate
SSM Partnership Formal partnership between ClearMinds Ltd and Smart Start Minds
Corporate Brand Market as "ClearMinds Corporate" without legal separation complexity
Resource Efficiency Shared platform, infrastructure, and team across B2C and Corporate
Cost Efficiency No company formation, no subsidiary admin, lower governance costs

Key Success Factors

  • Governance Board Established: Recruit Clinical Director, Chair, Commercial Director (see Governance Board Charter)
  • SSM Partnership Formalized: Partnership agreement between ClearMinds Ltd and Smart Start Minds
  • Corporate Division Clarity: Clear internal P&L and management structure for Corporate business
  • Professional Indemnity Insurance: Appropriate coverage for ClearMinds Ltd (covering both divisions)
  • Compliance Framework: Clinical Governance, Medical Compliance documentation in place

7. Governance Implications

7.1 Board Structure

ClearMinds Ltd Board:

  • Chair
  • Clinical Director (HCPC/GMC registered professional)
  • Commercial Director / Managing Director
  • Legal/Compliance Representative
  • Smart Start Minds Liaison (Dr. Thomas Dannhauser)

Board Responsibilities:

  • Governs entire company - both consumer product and Corporate division
  • Corporate partnerships require enhanced clinical governance (Board's primary focus)
  • Quarterly board meetings with clinical governance standing agenda item
  • Note: Enhanced governance standards benefit consumer product too

7.2 Clinical Governance

Clinical Director Role:

  • Accountable for clinical governance framework
  • Oversees content quality and clinical appropriateness
  • Reviews incidents involving user safety
  • Approves escalation protocols and SSM partnership arrangements
  • Reports to Board quarterly on clinical governance performance

Dr. Thomas Dannhauser / SSM Role:

  • SSM Liaison to Board (formal role)
  • Provides clinical pathway oversight and quality assurance
  • Reviews referral appropriateness and outcomes
  • Ensures CQC standards reflected in partnership
  • Participates in clinical governance discussions

7.3 Board Decision-Making

Decision Type ClearMinds Ltd Board Authority
Clinical Governance Framework ✅ Full authority to approve and enforce
New Corporate Partnerships ✅ Full authority (all sizes)
Annual Operating Budget ✅ Full authority for entire company
SSM Partnership Terms ✅ Full authority to negotiate and approve
Serious Incident Response ✅ Board oversight of all incidents
Staff Appointments ✅ Full authority for all roles

8. Future Thrive Integration

8.1 Thrive Roadmap Context

Thrive Vision:

Thrive is envisioned as an ambitious consumer wellness platform that will integrate:

  • Wellness assessments
  • OCEAN behavioral analysis
  • Personalized content and coaching
  • Community and social features
  • Potentially broader health and lifestyle integration

Current Status: Not yet launched, still in development/planning

8.2 Relationship Between ClearMinds Corporate and Thrive

Scenario Implication for ClearMinds Ltd
Thrive Launches as Consumer Brand ClearMinds Corporate continues independently; may license Thrive features if beneficial
Thrive Becomes Umbrella Brand ClearMinds Corporate could rebrand as "Thrive for Business" or "Thrive B2B" under umbrella
Thrive Acquires/Merges B2B ClearMinds Ltd merges into Thrive Ltd; governance transitions to Thrive Board
Separate Paths ClearMinds Corporate and Thrive remain independent entities serving different markets

8.3 Integration Flexibility

Key Advantage of Single Company Approach:

ClearMinds Ltd can evolve its relationship with Thrive flexibly:

  • License Thrive features when Thrive launches (if beneficial)
  • Rebrand Corporate offering as "Thrive Corporate" while maintaining ClearMinds Ltd entity
  • Partner with Thrive ecosystem as it develops
  • Maintain separate ClearMinds brand if that serves Corporate market better
  • Keep optionality open without complex subsidiary unwinding

This approach avoids premature legal separation while maintaining strategic flexibility.

8.4 Decision Criteria for Future Integration

Integration with Thrive should occur when:

  • Thrive brand is established and trusted in market
  • Thrive platform offers features beneficial for B2B (OCEAN, advanced assessments)
  • B2B partners comfortable with Thrive brand (not just ClearMinds)
  • Governance structures can be harmonized without losing B2B institutional rigor
  • Financial and operational integration makes strategic sense
  • SSM partnership can transfer cleanly to Thrive entity

Until then: ClearMinds Corporate operates independently, maintains governance standards, builds partnerships, and keeps options open.

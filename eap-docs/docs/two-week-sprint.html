This is the accelerated plan for teams with only 2 weeks until their partner meeting. It's intense but doable with focus and commitment. This sprint plan breaks down exactly what needs to happen each day, who should do it, and how to stay on track under extreme time pressure.

1. Critical Success Criteria

Success in this 14-day sprint is binary: either you deliver all five critical items and have a successful meeting, or you don't and the meeting fails. There's no middle ground with insurance partners who expect professional governance documentation.

The Five Non-Negotiables:

  1. Clinical Governance Framework (Document A): Complete documentation of how Thrive ensures user safety, content quality, and clinical oversight. Must be signed by Clinical Director or equivalent.
  2. Medical Compliance Statement (Document B): Clear positioning on regulatory status, medical device classification, disclaimers, and legal compliance. Legal review highly recommended but not mandatory if unavailable.
  3. Smart Start Minds Partnership (Document C): Verified partnership documentation showing CQC-regulated clinical pathway for escalation. Must be verified by SSM before presenting to partners.
  4. Evidence Base: Research citations, outcome data, and evidence supporting Thrive's effectiveness. This doesn't need to be a separate document but must be readily available to answer partner questions.
  5. Team Preparedness: Your team must know the documents inside and out, be able to answer tough questions, and present with confidence. No amount of documentation helps if your team fumbles the presentation.

Reality Check: This sprint assumes you work efficiently, hit deadlines, and don't encounter major blockers. If you're already behind, or if your team can't commit full-time to this sprint, you may need to request a meeting postponement. It's better to delay than to show up unprepared.

5. Emergency Protocols

IF THINGS GO WRONG:

Problem: Key information not received

Solution:

  • Use [PLACEHOLDER] and note "To be confirmed"
  • Acknowledge in meeting: "We're finalizing these details"
  • Commit to follow-up timeline

Problem: Legal review not available

Solution:

  • Proceed with best efforts on Document B
  • Note in document: "Legal review in progress"
  • Emphasize other strong elements (CQC pathway, governance)

Problem: SSM unresponsive

Solution:

  • Use publicly available CQC info (already have)
  • Document what you know, note what's being verified
  • Escalate contact attempts

Problem: Team member unavailable

Solution:

  • Redistribute work immediately (don't wait)
  • Consider bringing in backup resource
  • Work longer hours (unfortunate but necessary)

Problem: Meeting moved up

Solution:

  • Immediately assess readiness
  • Prioritize absolutely essential items
  • Request postponement if truly not ready (but avoid if possible)

ESCALATION:

If critical blocker arises:

  1. Flag immediately to project lead
  2. Assess impact on timeline
  3. Brainstorm solutions (team meeting)
  4. Escalate to senior leadership if needed
  5. Consider meeting postponement only as last resort

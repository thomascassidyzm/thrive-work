Thrive is committed to operating with the highest standards of integrity, transparency, and user safety. This document establishes our position regarding medical device classification, advertising standards, evidence-based practice, and regulatory compliance.

  • Maintain transparent communication with users, partners, and regulators
  • Conduct regular compliance audits and reviews

2. Regulatory Classification

2.1 NOT a Medical Device

Why Thrive Does NOT Meet Medical Device Definition

Under UK Medical Device Regulations 2002 (as amended) and MHRA guidance, a medical device must have a medical purpose including diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, treatment, or alleviation of disease.

Thrive does NOT meet this definition because:

  • Does not diagnose medical conditions: Thrive does not assess, identify, or diagnose mental health disorders or any medical conditions
  • Does not treat medical conditions: Thrive does not provide treatment for diagnosed mental health conditions or medical diseases
  • Does not prevent disease: Thrive does not claim to prevent the onset of mental health disorders or medical conditions
  • Does not monitor physiological processes: Thrive does not measure, monitor, or track physiological parameters for medical purposes

Regulatory Position: Wellness and personal development tool supporting general mental wellbeing, not a medical device.

Legal Basis: Our position is based on:

  • Medical Device Regulations 2002 (SI 2002/618, as amended)
  • MHRA Guidance on medical device stand-alone software (including apps)
  • EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR) 2017/745 (applicable during transition period)
  • [INSERT LEGAL OPINION REFERENCE if obtained]

Key Differentiators:

  • Thrive provides educational content, not medical advice
  • Users self-direct their use; no clinical assessment or diagnosis occurs
  • Content supports general wellness, not treatment of pathology
  • No medical claims made in marketing or user communications
  • Clear disclaimers inform users Thrive is not a substitute for medical care
Note: While Thrive is not a medical device, we maintain high standards of user safety, data protection, and ethical practice consistent with our commitment to user wellbeing. We monitor regulatory developments and will adjust our classification if guidance changes.

2.2 Positioning as Wellness Service

What Thrive IS:

  • Wellness and resilience-building platform: Tools and techniques to support mental wellbeing, stress management, and personal growth
  • Educational content on mental wellbeing: Evidence-based information about psychological wellbeing, resilience, mindfulness, and positive psychology
  • Self-help resources based on evidence-based principles: Interactive exercises, guided practices, and psycho-educational materials grounded in CBT, positive psychology, and mindfulness research

5.2 Limitations Acknowledged

Thrive is committed to transparency about the limitations of our evidence base and service. We openly acknowledge:

Research Limitations:

  • Translation from in-person to digital: Most foundational research (e.g., CBT, positive psychology) was conducted on in-person interventions. While digital adaptations show promise, outcomes may differ from face-to-face delivery.
  • Self-guided nature and variable engagement: Digital self-help tools depend on user engagement and self-direction. Research shows variable completion rates and outcomes based on individual motivation and circumstances.
  • Population differences: Research conducted on specific populations (e.g., clinical samples, students) may not generalize to all Thrive users (workplace employees with varied backgrounds and needs).

  • [OTHER LIMITATIONS - e.g., outcome measurement challenges, attribution difficulties in complex interventions, long-term follow-up data]

Transparency in Communications:

We communicate these limitations clearly:

  • No over-claiming: Marketing and user communications accurately represent what evidence does and doesn't show
  • Honest about unknowns: Where evidence is limited or uncertain, we acknowledge this rather than making unsupported claims

  • Ongoing evaluation communicated: Users and partners informed about our commitment to outcome measurement and continuous improvement
  • Individual variation acknowledged: Communications emphasize that outcomes vary based on individual circumstances, engagement, and needs
Commitment to Evidence: Despite limitations, Thrive remains committed to evidence-based practice. We continuously review emerging research, collect outcome data, and refine our approach based on best available evidence and user feedback.

  • Clear referral pathway: Thrive users can be referred to SSM for clinical assessment and treatment when wellness support is insufficient

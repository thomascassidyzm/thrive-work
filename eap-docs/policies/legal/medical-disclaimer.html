1. Nature of Our Services

1.1 What ClearMinds Provides

ClearMinds offers:

2. Not a Substitute for Medical Care

DO NOT use ClearMinds as a replacement for:

  • Your GP (General Practitioner)
  • Mental health professionals (psychiatrists, psychologists, therapists)
  • Medical specialists
  • Emergency services (999 or 111)
  • NHS mental health crisis services

2.1 When to Seek Medical Help

You MUST consult a qualified medical professional if you:

  • Have been diagnosed with a mental health condition (depression, anxiety disorder, PTSD, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, etc.)
  • Are currently receiving psychiatric treatment or taking psychiatric medication
  • Have suicidal thoughts or thoughts of self-harm
  • Experience psychotic symptoms (hallucinations, delusions)
  • Have a history of epilepsy or seizures
  • Are experiencing a mental health crisis
  • Have symptoms of a medical or psychological condition
  • Are pregnant and experiencing mental health difficulties
  • Are under the care of mental health services

2.2 Emergency Situations

If you are in crisis or experiencing a mental health emergency:

  • Call 999 if you or someone else is in immediate danger
  • Call 111 for urgent medical advice (NHS 111)
  • Contact Samaritans: 116 123 (24/7 free helpline)
  • Text SHOUT to 85258 for 24/7 crisis text support
  • Go to A&E or call your local mental health crisis team

DO NOT rely on ClearMinds in emergency situations.

3. Hypnotherapy Limitations and Contraindications

3.1 Not Suitable for Everyone

Hypnotherapy may not be appropriate for individuals with:

  • Epilepsy or seizure disorders – Relaxation techniques may trigger seizures in some individuals
  • Psychotic disorders – Including schizophrenia, schizoaffective disorder, or active psychosis
  • Severe depression – Particularly with suicidal ideation
  • Dissociative disorders – Including dissociative identity disorder or depersonalisation disorder
  • Certain personality disorders – Particularly those involving impaired reality testing
  • Active substance abuse or addiction – Without concurrent professional treatment
  • Recent trauma – Within the last 12 months, without professional psychological support

Important: If you have any of the above conditions, please consult your GP, psychiatrist, or mental health professional before using ClearMinds. Provide them with information about our hypnotherapy services and ask if it is safe and appropriate for you.

3.2 Complementary, Not Alternative, Medicine

Hypnotherapy is a complementary wellness practice, not an alternative to medical treatment:

  • Do not stop taking prescribed medication without consulting your doctor
  • Do not discontinue medical treatment in favour of hypnotherapy
  • Do not delay seeking medical advice because you are using ClearMinds
  • Always inform your healthcare providers that you are using hypnotherapy

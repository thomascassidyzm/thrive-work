2. Factors Affecting Results

2.1 Individual Differences

Your results from using ClearMinds may be influenced by many individual factors, including but not limited to:

  • Personal commitment and consistency – How regularly and consistently you use the services
  • Individual responsiveness to hypnotherapy – Some people are more responsive to hypnotic suggestion than others
  • Current mental and physical health status – Underlying health conditions may affect outcomes
  • Life circumstances – Stress levels, life events, social support, and environmental factors
  • Expectations and beliefs – Your mindset and openness to the process
  • Concurrent treatments or interventions – Other therapies, medications, or lifestyle changes
  • Age, health history, and genetics – Biological and developmental factors
  • Motivation and goals – Clarity of purpose and personal drive

2.2 Hypnotic Susceptibility

Important: Research shows that individuals vary widely in their responsiveness to hypnotic suggestion. Approximately 10-15% of people are highly susceptible to hypnosis, while 10-15% show minimal response. Most people fall somewhere in between. Your individual level of hypnotic susceptibility will significantly affect your experience and outcomes.

4. Required Effort and Commitment

4.1 Active Participation Required

Hypnotherapy is not a passive process. Results, if achieved, typically require:

  • Regular, consistent use – Daily or frequent practice over weeks or months
  • Active engagement – Full attention and participation during sessions
  • Openness and willingness – A receptive mindset and genuine desire for change
  • Integration of learnings – Applying insights and techniques in daily life
  • Patience and persistence – Allowing time for gradual changes to occur
  • Self-reflection and awareness – Monitoring your own thoughts, feelings, and behaviours

4.2 No "Quick Fix"

ClearMinds is not a "quick fix" or "miracle cure." While some users may notice benefits relatively quickly, lasting change typically requires sustained effort, practice, and personal commitment over an extended period.

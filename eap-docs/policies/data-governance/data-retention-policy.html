1. Purpose and Scope

This Data Retention Policy sets out how long ClearMinds retains personal data and the processes for reviewing and securely disposing of data when retention periods expire.

This policy is designed to ensure compliance with:

  • UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
  • Data Protection Act 2018
  • Professional clinical record-keeping standards
  • Financial and tax record requirements
  • Employment law obligations

The policy applies to all personal data processed by ClearMinds, whether in electronic or physical format.

2. Legal and Regulatory Framework

2.1 GDPR Storage Limitation Principle

Under UK GDPR Article 5(1)(e), personal data must be "kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data are processed."

2.2 Balancing Factors

We balance several considerations when determining retention periods:

  • Purpose: Why the data was collected and how long it's needed for that purpose
  • Legal Requirements: Minimum retention periods required by law or regulation
  • Clinical Best Practice: Professional standards for healthcare records
  • Legitimate Interests: Defence of legal claims, continuity of care
  • Data Subject Rights: Rights to erasure and other individual rights

4. Secure Disposal Procedures

When retention periods expire, ClearMinds ensures secure disposal of personal data:

4.1 Electronic Data

  • Secure Deletion: Multi-pass overwriting using approved deletion software
  • Backups: Deletion from all backup systems and archives
  • Cloud Storage: Deletion verified with service providers
  • Verification: Deletion confirmed and logged by IT team
  • Disposal Log: Record of what was deleted, when, and by whom

4.2 Physical Records

  • Confidential Shredding: Cross-cut shredding to DIN P-4 standard minimum
  • Secure Bins: Locked confidential waste bins
  • Certified Disposal: Use of DBS-checked disposal contractors
  • Destruction Certificates: Certificates of destruction obtained and retained
  • No Landfill: Shredded materials incinerated or recycled securely

4.3 Hardware and Devices

  • Degaussing: Magnetic media degaussed before disposal
  • Physical Destruction: Hard drives physically destroyed
  • WEEE Compliance: Electronic waste disposed of via certified WEEE contractors
  • Certificate of Destruction: Obtained for all hardware disposal

5. Exemptions and Extended Retention

Retention periods may be extended in the following circumstances:

5.1 Legal Hold

Data subject to legal proceedings, regulatory investigations, or formal complaints must be retained until the matter is resolved, regardless of normal retention periods.

5.2 Historical or Research Value

With appropriate safeguards and anonymisation, data may be retained longer for:

  • Legitimate research purposes (with ethical approval)
  • Statistical analysis and public health research
  • Audit and quality improvement

5.3 Safeguarding Concerns

Records involving serious safeguarding issues may be retained longer than standard periods to support historical abuse enquiries or ongoing risk management.

6. Review and Deletion Process

6.1 Automated Review

  • Automated systems flag records approaching retention period expiry
  • Quarterly reports generated for manual review
  • Notifications sent to relevant data owners

6.2 Manual Review

  • Data Protection Officer reviews flagged records
  • Assessment of any exemptions or extensions needed
  • Approval required before deletion of clinical records

6.3 Deletion Execution

  • Approved deletions executed by authorised IT personnel
  • Deletion logged with date, time, and responsible person
  • Verification of complete removal from all systems

7. Individual Rights and Retention

7.1 Right to Erasure Requests

Data subjects can request early deletion of their personal data. We will comply unless we have a legitimate reason to continue retention, such as:

  • Legal obligation to retain the data
  • Necessary for defence of legal claims
  • Necessary for public interest or research purposes (with safeguards)

7.2 Clinical Record Requests

Requests for early deletion of clinical records require careful assessment:

  • Clinical director must approve deletion of clinical records
  • Consideration of continuity of care implications
  • Consideration of safeguarding responsibilities
  • Assessment of legal and professional obligations
  • Documentation of decision-making process
Important: We cannot delete clinical records if there are ongoing safeguarding concerns, active legal proceedings, or regulatory investigations. Refusal to delete will be explained with reference to the specific legal basis.

8. Responsibilities

8.1 Data Protection Officer

  • Overall oversight of data retention compliance
  • Maintaining and updating retention schedules
  • Quarterly retention compliance reports
  • Reviewing and approving deletion requests

8.2 Clinical Director

  • Approving deletion of clinical records
  • Ensuring clinical record retention compliance
  • Advising on clinical retention considerations

8.3 IT Department

  • Implementing automated retention management systems
  • Executing approved deletions securely
  • Maintaining deletion logs
  • Managing secure disposal of hardware

8.4 All Staff

  • Complying with retention requirements
  • Not retaining data beyond approved periods
  • Using secure disposal procedures
  • Reporting retention issues to the DPO

9. Monitoring and Auditing

  • Quarterly Reviews: Compliance with retention schedules
  • Annual Audits: Full audit of retention practices
  • Deletion Logs: Review of deletion records for completeness
  • Spot Checks: Random sampling of records to verify compliance
  • External Audits: Periodic independent assessment

  • Induction training covering retention policies
  • Annual refresher training
  • Role-specific training for records management
  • Updates on policy changes

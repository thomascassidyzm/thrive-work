üìß Sending Sensitive Information by Email

STOP! Is this email secure? Before sending personal data:
  1. Check the recipient: Is the email address correct?
  2. Encrypt if sensitive: Use encryption for health data
  3. Use BCC for bulk emails: Don't expose everyone's email address
  4. Consider alternatives: Could you use secure portal instead?

Common Email Mistakes to Avoid:

  • ‚ùå Using "Reply All" when you should "Reply"
  • ‚ùå Auto-complete selecting the wrong recipient
  • ‚ùå Forgetting to remove previous recipients from a forward
  • ‚ùå Including personal data in the subject line
  • ‚ùå Sending unencrypted clinical notes

üîê Password Security

Strong Password Requirements:

  • Minimum 12 characters
  • Mix of upper & lowercase
  • Include numbers & symbols
  • Never reuse passwords
  • Use a password manager

Password DON'Ts:

  • Don't use personal info
  • Don't write them down
  • Don't share with colleagues
  • Don't use same password twice
  • Don't save in browser

üö® Recognizing a Data Breach

A data breach is ANY incident where personal data is:
  • Lost or stolen (laptop, USB, papers)
  • Accessed by unauthorized person
  • Sent to wrong person
  • Disclosed accidentally
  • Altered or corrupted
  • Made unavailable

If You Suspect a Data Breach:

  1. STOP - Don't make it worse
  2. CONTAIN - Prevent further access if possible
  3. REPORT IMMEDIATELY - Contact DPO at dpo@clearmindseap.com or [EMERGENCY NUMBER]
  4. PRESERVE EVIDENCE - Don't delete anything
  5. DOCUMENT - Write down exactly what happened

üì± Mobile Device & Remote Working

  • Enable device encryption and passcode
  • Use VPN when working remotely
  • Never leave devices unattended in public

üóëÔ∏è Disposing of Data Securely

Physical Records:

  • Use confidential waste bins
  • Cross-cut shred all documents
  • Never put in regular bin
  • Lock confidential waste bins

Electronic Records:

  • Delete files properly (not just recycle bin)
  • Contact IT for hard drive disposal
  • Wipe devices before disposal
  • Never throw away USB/hard drives

üë• Client Confidentiality (Clinical Staff)

Remember: Client confidentiality is ABSOLUTE unless:
  • Client gives explicit consent to share
  • There's a safeguarding concern
  • There's a serious risk of harm to client or others
  • You're legally required to disclose (court order)

Clinical Best Practices:

  • ‚úì Never discuss clients in public areas
  • ‚úì Use client reference numbers, not names, in non-clinical systems
  • ‚úì Lock clinical records when not in use
  • ‚úì Ensure video session backgrounds are neutral
  • ‚úì Close door/window when speaking with clients
  • ‚úì Don't leave voicemails with clinical details

‚ùì Common Questions

Q: Can I take work home?

A: Only with manager approval, on encrypted devices, and following remote working policy.

Q: Can I use my personal email for work?

A: No. Always use your ClearMinds email for work communications.

Q: Can I use WhatsApp to message colleagues about work?

A: Only for general coordination. NEVER share personal data or clinical information via WhatsApp.

Q: What if a client asks me to email their records?

A: Direct them to make a Subject Access Request (SAR) via the DPO. Don't email clinical records directly.

Q: Can I share anonymised case studies in training?

A: Only if truly anonymised (no way to identify individual) or with explicit written consent. Check with DPO if unsure.

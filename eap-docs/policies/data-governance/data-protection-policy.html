1. Introduction and Purpose

ClearMinds is committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and all applicable data protection legislation.

As a provider of Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) and mental health support services, we process sensitive personal data, including special category data relating to health. We recognise the critical importance of maintaining the highest standards of data protection and privacy.

This policy sets out our approach to data protection and the responsibilities of all staff, contractors, and partners who process personal data on behalf of ClearMinds.

2. Scope and Application

This policy applies to:

  • All personal data processed by ClearMinds
  • All staff members, contractors, consultants, and temporary workers
  • All data processing activities, whether manual or automated
  • All locations where ClearMinds operates
  • All third-party processors acting on behalf of ClearMinds

3. Key Definitions

Term Definition
Personal Data Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual
Special Category Data Sensitive data including health data, racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, sexual orientation, trade union membership, genetic/biometric data

Data Processor An organisation that processes personal data on behalf of the data controller

ICO Information Commissioner's Office - the UK's independent data protection authority

4. Data Protection Principles

ClearMinds adheres to the seven key principles of data protection as set out in the UK GDPR:

1. Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency

We process personal data lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner. We clearly inform data subjects about how we use their data.

2. Purpose Limitation

We collect personal data for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes. We do not process data in a manner incompatible with those purposes.

3. Data Minimisation

We only collect personal data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the purposes for which it is processed.

4. Accuracy

We take reasonable steps to ensure personal data is accurate and kept up to date. Inaccurate data is erased or rectified without delay.

5. Storage Limitation

We keep personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, in accordance with our Data Retention Policy.

6. Integrity and Confidentiality (Security)

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

7. Accountability

We take responsibility for complying with data protection legislation and can demonstrate our compliance through policies, procedures, training, and documentation.

5. Lawful Basis for Processing

ClearMinds processes personal data under the following lawful bases:

5.1 Standard Personal Data

  • Consent: For processing with explicit, informed consent from the data subject
  • Contract: For processing necessary to fulfil contractual obligations with service users
  • Legal Obligation: For processing required to comply with legal requirements
  • Legitimate Interests: For processing necessary for legitimate business interests, balanced against data subject rights

5.2 Special Category Data (Health Data)

For health-related data, we rely on:

  • Explicit Consent: Clear, informed consent for processing health data to provide mental health services
  • Health and Social Care: Processing necessary for health or social care purposes under Article 9(2)(h) UK GDPR
  • Substantial Public Interest: Where applicable under Schedule 1 of the Data Protection Act 2018
Important: All processing of special category data must be documented with clear justification and appropriate safeguards in place. Staff must not process health data without proper authorisation and training.

6. Data Subject Rights

ClearMinds respects and facilitates the following rights of data subjects under UK GDPR:

6.1 Right to Be Informed

Data subjects have the right to clear information about what personal data we collect and how we use it. We provide this through our Privacy Policy and privacy notices.

6.2 Right of Access (Subject Access Request)

Data subjects can request a copy of their personal data. We respond to Subject Access Requests (SARs) within one month. See our SAR Procedure for details.

6.3 Right to Rectification

Data subjects can request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. We action such requests within one month.

6.4 Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten")

Data subjects can request deletion of their personal data in certain circumstances, subject to legal retention requirements and legitimate interests.

6.5 Right to Restrict Processing

Data subjects can request restriction of processing in specific situations, such as when accuracy is contested or processing is unlawful.

6.6 Right to Data Portability

Data subjects can request their personal data in a structured, commonly used format and have it transferred to another controller where technically feasible.

6.7 Right to Object

Data subjects can object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

6.8 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making

Data subjects have rights regarding automated decision-making and profiling. ClearMinds does not currently use fully automated decision-making processes.

7. Data Security Measures

ClearMinds implements robust security measures to protect personal data:

7.1 Technical Security

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Secure authentication and access controls (MFA required)
  • Regular security updates and patch management
  • Firewall protection and intrusion detection systems
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Secure backup procedures with encrypted storage
  • Anti-virus and anti-malware protection

7.2 Organisational Security

  • Role-based access controls (principle of least privilege)
  • Mandatory data protection training for all staff
  • Clear desk and clear screen policies
  • Secure disposal of physical records (confidential shredding)
  • Visitor management and physical access controls
  • Confidentiality agreements for all staff and contractors
  • Regular review of security policies and procedures

7.3 Data Access Controls

  • Access to personal data restricted to authorised personnel only
  • Individual user accounts with unique credentials
  • Logging and monitoring of data access
  • Regular access rights reviews
  • Immediate revocation of access upon termination of employment

9. Data Protection by Design and Default

ClearMinds embeds data protection into all business processes and systems from the outset:

  • Privacy by Design: Data protection considerations integrated into the design of new systems, services, and processes
  • Privacy by Default: Default settings provide the highest level of privacy protection
  • Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs): Conducted for high-risk processing activities
  • Vendor Assessment: Due diligence on third-party processors before engagement
  • Regular Reviews: Ongoing assessment of data processing activities and risks

10. Data Sharing and Third-Party Processing

10.1 Third-Party Processors

When engaging third-party processors, ClearMinds ensures:

  • A written Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is in place
  • The processor provides sufficient guarantees of data security
  • The processor only processes data on our documented instructions
  • Regular audits or assessments of processor compliance
  • Clear procedures for sub-processor engagement

10.2 International Data Transfers

ClearMinds processes data primarily within the UK. Any international transfers are:

  • Subject to appropriate safeguards (adequacy decisions, Standard Contractual Clauses, or other approved mechanisms)
  • Documented and risk-assessed
  • Compliant with UK GDPR Chapter V requirements

10.3 Data Sharing with B2B Partners

For corporate partners (insurance companies, employers), data sharing is governed by:

  • Written agreements specifying data sharing scope and limitations
  • Clear legal basis for sharing (typically contract or legitimate interests)
  • Minimum necessary data shared (data minimisation)
  • Secure transfer methods (encrypted channels)
  • Regular review of data sharing arrangements

11. Data Breach Management

ClearMinds has established procedures for identifying, reporting, and responding to data breaches. See our Data Breach Response Plan for full details.

11.1 Breach Identification

  • All staff trained to recognise potential data breaches
  • Multiple reporting channels available
  • 24/7 incident reporting capability

11.2 Breach Response

  • Immediate containment and mitigation measures
  • Investigation and risk assessment
  • ICO notification within 72 hours (if required)
  • Data subject notification (if high risk to rights and freedoms)
  • Post-incident review and corrective actions

12. Training and Awareness

ClearMinds provides comprehensive data protection training:

  • Induction Training: All new staff receive data protection training before accessing personal data
  • Annual Refresher Training: Mandatory for all staff
  • Role-Specific Training: Additional training for staff handling sensitive data
  • Awareness Campaigns: Regular communications on data protection topics
  • Testing and Evaluation: Training effectiveness monitored through assessments

13. Records and Documentation

ClearMinds maintains comprehensive records of data processing activities, including:

  • Record of Processing Activities (ROPA): Detailed inventory of all processing activities
  • Data Protection Impact Assessments: For high-risk processing
  • Legitimate Interest Assessments: Where applicable
  • Data Processing Agreements: With all processors
  • Privacy Notices: Current and archived versions
  • Consent Records: Where consent is the lawful basis
  • Data Breach Log: All breaches, including those not reported to the ICO
  • Training Records: Staff training completion and dates

14. Monitoring and Review

This policy and our data protection practices are subject to regular review:

  • Annual Policy Review: Full review of all data protection policies
  • Quarterly Audits: Internal audits of data protection compliance
  • Continuous Monitoring: Ongoing monitoring of data processing activities
  • Incident Analysis: Review of data breaches and near misses
  • Regulatory Updates: Monitoring of legal and regulatory changes
  • External Audits: Periodic independent assessment of compliance

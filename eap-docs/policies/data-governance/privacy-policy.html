3. Information We Collect

We collect different types of information depending on how you interact with our services:

3.1 Information You Provide Directly

Account and Registration Information

  • Name and contact details (email, phone number)
  • Date of birth
  • Employer/organisation name (for EAP access)
  • Employee ID or reference number
  • Relationship to the organisation (employee, family member, etc.)

Health and Wellbeing Information

  • Information about your mental health and wellbeing concerns
  • Details of counselling sessions and therapy notes
  • Assessment questionnaires and screening results
  • Treatment plans and progress notes
  • Medication information (if relevant)
  • Information about other health conditions that may affect your treatment
  • Family history (where relevant to your care)

Communication Information

  • Records of your communications with us (calls, emails, chat messages)
  • Feedback and survey responses
  • Complaints or concerns you raise

3.2 Information We Collect Automatically

Technical and Usage Information

  • Device information (type, operating system, browser)
  • IP address and approximate location
  • Login times and session duration
  • Pages visited and features used
  • Referral source (how you found our service)

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to improve your experience. See our Cookie Policy for detailed information.

3.3 Information from Third Parties

  • From Your Employer: Limited information to verify your eligibility for EAP services (usually just employment status and employee reference)
  • From Healthcare Providers: With your explicit consent, we may receive information from your GP or other healthcare providers
  • From Insurance Companies: If your service is provided through an insurance scheme, we may receive verification information

4. How We Use Your Information

We only use your personal information for specific, legitimate purposes:

4.1 Providing Mental Health Services

Legal Basis: Explicit consent and health/social care purposes

  • Assessing your needs and providing appropriate support
  • Delivering counselling, therapy, and mental health interventions
  • Monitoring your progress and adapting treatment plans
  • Coordinating care with other healthcare providers (with your consent)
  • Providing crisis support and risk management

4.2 Service Administration

Legal Basis: Contract and legitimate interests

  • Creating and managing your account
  • Scheduling and managing appointments
  • Processing payments (if applicable)
  • Sending service-related communications (appointment reminders, updates)
  • Responding to your enquiries and requests

4.3 EAP Partner Reporting

Legal Basis: Contract and legitimate interests

  • Providing anonymised, aggregated usage statistics to your employer/insurance provider
  • Confirming service utilisation for billing purposes
  • Reporting on service effectiveness (anonymised data only)
Important: We never share details of your sessions or the nature of your concerns with your employer. They only receive anonymised statistical information about overall service usage.

4.4 Service Improvement and Research

Legal Basis: Legitimate interests and consent (where required)

  • Analysing service usage to improve our offerings
  • Conducting anonymised research to enhance mental health support
  • Quality assurance and clinical supervision
  • Training and development of practitioners (anonymised or with explicit consent)

4.5 Legal and Safety Obligations

Legal Basis: Legal obligation and vital interests

  • Complying with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Safeguarding children and vulnerable adults
  • Preventing serious harm to you or others
  • Responding to lawful requests from authorities
  • Defending legal claims

5. Who We Share Your Information With

We share your personal information only when necessary and with appropriate safeguards:

5.1 Healthcare Professionals

  • Our Clinicians: Counsellors, therapists, and mental health professionals providing your care
  • Clinical Supervisors: For quality assurance and professional development (anonymised or with consent)
  • Other Healthcare Providers: Your GP or other healthcare professionals (only with your explicit consent)
  • Emergency Services: In crisis situations where there's a risk of serious harm

5.2 Your Employer / Insurance Provider

  • What We Share: Only anonymised, aggregated statistics about service usage
  • What We DON'T Share: Any details about your individual sessions, concerns, or treatment
  • Verification: Confirmation that you've accessed the service (for billing purposes only)

5.3 Technology and Service Providers

  • Hosting Providers: Secure cloud infrastructure (AWS, Azure)
  • Video Platform: For secure telehealth sessions
  • Communication Services: Email, SMS, and appointment reminders
  • Payment Processors: For secure payment handling (if applicable)
  • IT Support: Technical maintenance and support (with strict confidentiality agreements)

All service providers are carefully vetted and bound by Data Processing Agreements ensuring they meet GDPR standards.

5.4 Legal and Regulatory Authorities

  • When legally required (court orders, regulatory investigations)
  • To protect vital interests (preventing serious harm)
  • For safeguarding purposes (child or vulnerable adult protection)
  • To defend legal claims

6. International Data Transfers

We primarily store and process your data within the United Kingdom. If we need to transfer data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Transfers only to countries with an adequacy decision from the UK government
  • Use of Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the ICO
  • Additional technical and organisational security measures
  • Assessment of legal protections in the destination country

8. Your Rights and Choices

Under UK GDPR, you have important rights regarding your personal information:

8.1 Right to Access (Subject Access Request)

You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. We'll provide this within one month, free of charge.

8.2 Right to Rectification

You can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information. We'll update records within one month.

8.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten")

You can request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances. However, we may need to retain some information for:

  • Legal and regulatory requirements
  • Clinical record-keeping obligations
  • Defending legal claims
  • Safeguarding purposes

8.4 Right to Restrict Processing

You can ask us to restrict processing of your data in specific situations, such as when you contest the accuracy of the data.

8.5 Right to Data Portability

You can request your personal data in a structured, commonly used format to transfer to another service provider.

8.6 Right to Object

You can object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. We'll stop processing unless we have compelling legitimate grounds.

8.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making

You have rights regarding automated decision-making and profiling. We don't currently use fully automated decision-making for clinical purposes.

8.8 Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time. This won't affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

9. How We Protect Your Information

We implement robust security measures to protect your personal data:

9.1 Technical Security

  • Encryption: Data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Access Controls: Multi-factor authentication and role-based access
  • Secure Infrastructure: UK-based, ISO 27001 certified data centres
  • Regular Testing: Penetration testing and security audits
  • Monitoring: 24/7 security monitoring and threat detection
  • Backups: Regular encrypted backups with secure storage

9.2 Organisational Security

  • Staff Training: Mandatory data protection and confidentiality training
  • Confidentiality: All staff bound by professional and contractual confidentiality obligations
  • Need-to-Know: Access to data limited to those who need it for their role
  • Clear Policies: Comprehensive policies for handling personal data
  • Regular Audits: Internal and external compliance audits

9.3 Physical Security

  • Secure premises with access controls
  • Locked cabinets for physical records
  • Confidential shredding for document disposal
  • Clear desk and clear screen policies

11. Children's Privacy

We may provide services to children and young people under 18. When we do:

  • We seek appropriate parental/guardian consent for children under 13
  • For young people aged 13-17, we assess their capacity to consent (Gillick competence)
  • We involve parents/guardians appropriately while respecting the young person's confidentiality
  • We retain children's records until their 25th birthday (minimum)
  • We have enhanced safeguarding procedures in place

Disciplinary Procedure

Fair & Transparent Conduct Management

Purpose

This procedure ensures fair, consistent handling of conduct and performance issues, complying with ACAS Code of Practice.

Scope

Applies to misconduct including: poor timekeeping, policy breaches, inappropriate behaviour, insubordination. Serious matters may result in summary dismissal.

Informal Action

Minor issues addressed through informal discussion before formal action. Documented with improvement expectations and support offered.

Formal Procedure

Investigation: Facts gathered impartially. Employee may be suspended (with pay) during investigation if necessary.

Disciplinary Hearing: Written notice (5 days), right to be accompanied, opportunity to respond, decision communicated in writing within 5 days.

Outcomes

  • Verbal Warning: Recorded, valid for 6 months
  • Written Warning: Formal record, valid for 12 months
  • Final Written Warning: Valid for 12-24 months
  • Dismissal: With notice (or summary for gross misconduct)

Gross Misconduct Examples

  • Theft or fraud
  • Violence or threatening behaviour
  • Serious breach of health & safety
  • Harassment or discrimination
  • Being under influence of drugs/alcohol
  • Serious negligence causing significant harm
  • Serious breach of confidentiality

Right to Appeal

Appeals must be submitted within 5 working days. Heard by manager not previously involved. Decision final.

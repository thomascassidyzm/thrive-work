Employee Handbook

Your Complete Guide to Working at ClearMinds

Welcome to ClearMinds

This handbook provides everything you need to know about working at ClearMinds. Please read it carefully and refer back whenever you have questions. All employees are expected to understand and follow these policies.

1. About ClearMinds

Our Mission

To empower individuals to take control of their mental, emotional, and physical wellbeing through accessible, expert-led hypnotherapy.

Our Vision

A world where lasting personal change is possible for everyone, supported by science-backed tools and a caring, compassionate community.

Our Values

  • Evidence-Based: Grounded in research and clinical expertise
  • Accessible: Wellbeing support for all, not just the privileged few
  • Compassionate: Empathy and understanding in everything we do
  • Empowering: Helping people take control of their own wellbeing
  • Inclusive: Welcoming diversity and celebrating differences

2. Employment Basics

Your Contract

Your written contract sets out: job title, salary, hours, notice period, probation period. Read carefully and raise any questions with HR.

Probation Period

  • 6 months for most roles (3 months for senior positions)
  • Regular reviews at 1, 3, and 5 months
  • Extended if concerns, or confirmed early if excelling
  • 1 week notice during probation (either party)

Working Hours

Standard: 37.5 hours/week (Monday-Friday, 9am-5:30pm with 1 hour lunch). Flexibility available (see Hybrid Working Policy). Core hours: 10am-4pm (must be available).

Pay

  • Monthly salary paid last working day of month
  • Payslips via online portal
  • Annual salary review (April)
  • Pension: 5% employee, 3% employer (auto-enrolment)

3. Benefits

Annual Leave

  • 28 days (including bank holidays)
  • Increases to 30 days after 5 years
  • Buy/sell up to 5 days per year

Wellbeing

  • Free ClearMinds Premium membership
  • 3 free family memberships
  • Mental Health First Aiders
  • Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)
  • Cycle to Work scheme
  • Eye test vouchers

Development

  • £1,000 annual training budget
  • 5 days study leave
  • Professional membership fees paid
  • Internal mentoring programme

Social

  • Monthly team lunches
  • Quarterly company events
  • Annual summer party and winter party
  • Volunteer days (2 per year)

4. Your Responsibilities

Conduct & Behaviour

Expected to:

  • Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect
  • Act professionally at all times
  • Follow company policies and procedures
  • Protect confidential information
  • Report concerns about misconduct or safety

Confidentiality & Data Protection

  • User data is highly sensitive - handle with care
  • Never access user accounts without authorisation
  • Don't share passwords or leave systems logged in
  • Report data breaches immediately
  • Complete annual GDPR training

Health & Safety

  • Take reasonable care of your own safety and others
  • Report hazards, accidents, or near-misses
  • Don't come to work if contagiously unwell
  • DSE assessment required for all desk workers
  • Fire drills taken seriously

5. Key Policies (Summary)

Absence

  • Notify manager by 9:30am on first day
  • Self-certification up to 7 days
  • Fit note required for 8+ days
  • Company sick pay: full pay 4 weeks, half pay 4 weeks

Disciplinary & Grievance

Formal procedures exist for conduct issues and raising concerns. See full policies for details. Right to be accompanied at formal meetings.

Equality & Diversity

Zero tolerance for discrimination, harassment, or bullying. Everyone treated fairly regardless of protected characteristics.

Flexible & Hybrid Working

Flexible working requests considered sympathetically. Default hybrid: 2-3 days office, 2-3 days remote. Wednesdays are team days (all in office).

6. Career Development

Performance Reviews

  • Annual appraisal (goal-setting, salary review)
  • Mid-year review (progress check)
  • Monthly 1-2-1s (ongoing feedback)

Promotion

  • Internal opportunities advertised first
  • Annual promotion round (May)
  • Based on performance, potential, business need
  • Typical time in role: 18-24 months before promotion

Career Conversations

Discuss aspirations openly with your manager. HR happy to discuss career options confidentially.

7. Leaving ClearMinds

Resignation

  • Written notice required (email to manager and HR)
  • Notice period: 1 month (or as per contract)
  • Exit interview offered
  • Equipment returned, accounts closed
  • Final pay includes unused holiday

Redundancy

Fair selection process, consultation period, redundancy pay as per statutory minimums (or better), outplacement support offered.

8. Important Contacts

  • HR Team: hr@clearminds.com
  • IT Support: it@clearminds.com
  • Facilities: facilities@clearminds.com
  • Confidential Concerns: whistleblowing@clearminds.com
  • Emergency: Call 999, then notify manager/HR

Questions?

Your manager and HR team are here to help. Don't hesitate to ask if anything is unclear. We want you to feel supported and informed.

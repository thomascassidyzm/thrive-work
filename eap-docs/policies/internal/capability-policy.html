Capability Policy

Supporting Performance Improvement

Policy Information

Policy Owner: HR Director

Effective Date: July 2025

Review Date: July 2026

1. Purpose

This policy provides a fair, supportive framework for managing situations where an employee's performance or capability does not meet the required standards. Our aim is to help employees improve through clear expectations, appropriate support, and structured development plans.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees and covers capability issues including lack of skills, knowledge, aptitude, or health-related performance concerns (not willful misconduct, which is covered under the Disciplinary Procedure).

3. Informal Support

Before formal procedures, managers should:

  • Have honest conversations about performance concerns
  • Provide clear examples and expectations
  • Offer training, mentoring, or additional support
  • Allow reasonable time for improvement
  • Document discussions and agreed actions

4. Formal Capability Procedure

4.1 Investigation

If informal support doesn't resolve concerns, a formal review investigates the capability issues, gathering relevant evidence and documentation.

4.2 Capability Meeting

Employees will be:

  • Given at least 5 working days' written notice
  • Informed of specific concerns
  • Entitled to be accompanied by a colleague or trade union representative
  • Given opportunity to respond and provide evidence

4.3 Outcomes

Possible outcomes include:

  • No action: If performance is satisfactory
  • First written warning: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) with 3-month review period
  • Final written warning: If improvement insufficient, extended PIP with closer monitoring
  • Dismissal: As a last resort if performance remains inadequate despite support

5. Performance Improvement Plans

PIPs will include:

  • Specific, measurable performance objectives
  • Support provided (training, mentoring, resources)
  • Regular review meetings (typically fortnightly)
  • Clear timescales for improvement
  • Consequences if improvement not achieved

6. Health-Related Capability

Where ill health affects capability:

  • Occupational health referral will be arranged
  • Reasonable adjustments considered
  • Alternative roles explored if appropriate
  • Medical retirement options discussed if applicable
  • Full pay maintained during assessment period (up to 3 months)

7. Right to Appeal

Employees may appeal any formal outcome within 5 working days. Appeals will be heard by a more senior manager not previously involved.

Our Commitment

ClearMinds believes in developing our people. This policy exists to support improvement, not to punish. We will always provide fair opportunity and appropriate support before considering any employment termination.

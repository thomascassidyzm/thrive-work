2. Crisis Management Protocols

Crisis Categories & Frequency (Past 12 Months)

Crisis Type Frequency Response Protocol
Suicidal ideation posts 3-5 incidents Immediate removal + DM + helpline
Self-harm posts 5-8 incidents Immediate removal + DM + resources
Severe distress (psychosis, breakdown) 5-10 incidents Remove + DM + escalate to team lead

⚠️ Important Context: Across 91,000 members with 31,507 active users, crisis incidents represent 0.01-0.02% of community activity. Zero members have harmed themselves after posting (to our knowledge based on family contacts and follow-ups).

Step-by-Step Crisis Response

When Crisis Content is Posted:

Step 1: Immediate Removal
Post is removed within minutes to prevent:
  • Triggering other vulnerable members
  • Contagion effect in the community
  • Public visibility of private crisis
Step 2: Direct Message (Always)
Moderator sends compassionate DM containing:
  • Acknowledgment: "You are not alone, help is available"
  • Crisis helpline numbers (Samaritans 116 123, emergency services 999/911)
  • Reassurance: "We will follow up within 24 hours"
  • clear minds support contact (info@clearminds.com)
Step 3: Internal Escalation
Moderator immediately:
  • Notifies team lead (Head of Customer Care)
  • Documents incident in incident log with timestamp
  • Flags for 24-hour follow-up
Step 4: 24-Hour Follow-Up
Team checks back to see if member:
  • Responded to DM (if yes, provide ongoing support)
  • Is still active in community (monitor for follow-up posts)
  • Needs additional resources

Emergency Services Contact Protocol

Policy: We do NOT contact emergency services directly on behalf of members.

Rationale:

  • We respect user privacy and confidentiality
  • We don't have verified location/contact information
  • We are not medical professionals qualified to assess imminent risk
  • Breaching privacy could damage trust and deter help-seeking

Exception: If we saw an immediate reason or person in an extremely vulnerable state or crisis, we would urge them to contact emergency helplines. We do not want to breach or violate privacy and data policies sharing personal details or information like their address or phone number.

Family Contact: In some cases, family members have contacted us on behalf of a loved one struggling with thoughts of self-harm and/or suicide. We respond immediately with resources and support guidance.

Track Record: Emergency services contacted directly: 0 times in past 12 months across 91,000 members.

5. Policies & Enforcement

Community Guidelines (13 Rules)

✓Written rules posted in Facebook group - Full document available separately

Prohibited Content

  • ✓ Medical advice (diagnosing, prescribing)
  • ✓ Promotion of self-harm or pro-ED content
  • ✓ Spam or commercial promotion
  • ✓ Harassment or personal attacks
  • ✓ Sharing private information (doxxing)
  • ✓ Political/religious debates
  • ✓ Graphic content (self-harm images, etc.)

Enforcement Process

First Offense

  • Warning (private message)
  • Post removal
  • Temporary restriction (depending on severity)
  • Immediate ban for serious violations

Repeat Offenses

  • Second warning
  • Temporary ban (3-7 days depending on severity)
  • Permanent ban if excessive

Serious Violations (Threats, Illegal Content)

  • Immediate permanent ban
  • Report to Facebook
  • Report to authorities if legally required

Documentation

✓ All enforcement actions are documented with:

  • Timestamp
  • User identifier (anonymized in logs)
  • Violation type
  • Action taken
  • Moderator who handled

7. B2B Adaptations for Corporate Deployment

Key Differences: B2C vs B2B Community

Privacy Requirements

B2C (Current): Members use real names, share freely

B2B (Corporate): Mandatory anonymity, employer non-participation enforced

Employer Boundaries

  • Custom reporting: Provide anonymized engagement and wellbeing trend reports to employers (aggregated stats only)
  • Professional context: Moderators trained in workplace-specific sensitivities and corporate wellness communication
  • No employer visibility: Technically enforced - employers cannot see which employees are members

Content Moderation Adjustments

What changes for B2B:

  • Work stress allowed: Employees can share work-related stress (emotional support context)
  • Employer naming prohibited: Cannot name employer or colleagues (privacy violation, prevents grievance forum)
  • Workplace conflicts: Redirect to HR/EAP counseling, not community discussion
  • Professional tone: Maintain supportive but workplace-appropriate language

Dedicated Moderation Team for B2B (Optional)

For large corporate deployments (e.g., Towergate 250k employees), consider:

  • Dedicated B2B moderators: 1-2 moderators specifically trained for workplace wellness communities
  • Separate community space: In-app communities with company segmentation (vs external Facebook)
  • Enhanced employer reporting: Quarterly wellbeing trend analysis (anonymized)

Employer Non-Snooping Agreement

✓ Contractual prohibition with enforceable breach penalties

✓ Technical controls prevent employer access to member list

✓ Audit trail if employer attempts to identify employees

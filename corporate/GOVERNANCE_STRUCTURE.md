# ClearMinds Governance Structure
## Board Oversight & Advisory Framework

---

## Overview

ClearMinds operates a **two-tier governance structure** ensuring strategic oversight, operational excellence, and continuous quality improvement:

1. **Board of Directors** – Strategic direction, risk management, regulatory compliance
2. **Advisory Board** – Clinical quality, content oversight, customer feedback integration

This structure ensures that ClearMinds is managed in accordance with its established strategy while remaining compliant with external standards and responsive to user needs.

---

## Board of Directors

### Role & Mandate

The **Board of Directors** acts as the governing body for ClearMinds Corporate, with direct oversight and jurisdiction over:

- **Strategic Direction**: Three-year business plan, market positioning, growth targets
- **Governance Framework**: Approval of all policies, procedures, and compliance frameworks
- **Risk Management**: Oversight of enterprise risk register, incident reporting, crisis management
- **Financial Oversight**: Budget approval, investment decisions, revenue targets
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Ensuring adherence to GDPR, consumer protection, safeguarding principles
- **Stakeholder Accountability**: Representing shareholder/founder interests, B2B client commitments

### Key Responsibilities

1. **Approve Governance Documentation**: All policies, SLAs, and frameworks require board sign-off
2. **Mandate Advisory Board**: Delegates operational oversight to Advisory Board, receives quarterly reports
3. **Monitor Performance**: Reviews KPIs quarterly (user engagement, satisfaction, compliance metrics, B2B client retention)
4. **Regulatory Positioning**: Decides on CQC registration status, certification roadmap (ISO 27001, Cyber Essentials Plus), international expansion
5. **Crisis Escalation**: Receives immediate notification of Priority 0 incidents (data breaches, safeguarding failures, regulatory investigations)

## Advisory Board

### Role & Mandate

The **Advisory Board** reports to the Board of Directors with responsibility for:

- **Content Quality Assurance**: Clinical accuracy, evidence-based practices, therapeutic efficacy
- **Practitioner Oversight**: Audit qualifications, performance, clinical supervision compliance
- **Legislative Monitoring**: Track GDPR, safeguarding, consumer protection law changes
- **Customer Feedback**: Integrate user insights, NPS analysis, complaint themes into continuous improvement
- **Innovation & Development**: Recommend new therapeutic content, technology enhancements, research partnerships

### Key Responsibilities

#### 1. Content Quality Assurance

- **Review all therapeutic content** for clinical accuracy before release
- **Ensure evidence-based practices**: Align hypnotherapy scripts with current clinical guidelines
- **Monitor user feedback**: Track session completion rates, user ratings, efficacy reports
- **Approve new content**: No therapeutic audio goes live without Advisory Board clinical review
- **Quarterly content audits**: Sample review of existing library for continued relevance and safety

#### 2. Practitioner Oversight

- **Audit practitioner qualifications**: All content creators must hold minimum Diploma-level hypnotherapy certification
- **Oversee clinical supervision compliance**: For practitioners with CQC-regulated roles (via partner network)
- **Investigate quality concerns**: User complaints about content quality, safety, or appropriateness
- **Recommend training or remediation**: Professional development for content creators
- **Annual practitioner review**: Performance assessment based on user feedback and content quality metrics

#### 3. Community Safety & Crisis Management

- **Oversee community moderation**: Review 24/7 crisis response performance (91,000-member community, proven 4-year safety record)
- **Monitor crisis protocols**: Ensure <10 minute response time to suicidal ideation, self-harm, and severe distress posts
- **Safety record accountability**: Maintain zero-incident record through professional moderation and escalation procedures
- **Scalability oversight**: Prepare for enterprise deployment (250,000+ users for Towergate contract)
- **Family support protocols**: Ensure moderators follow safe procedures when family members contact about loved ones in crisis
- **Quarterly crisis audit**: Review anonymized crisis incident reports, identify improvement opportunities, update response templates
- **Integration with clinical pathways**: Ensure seamless referral from community crisis support (Pathway 1) to Concierge Health Advisors clinical services (Pathway 3)

**Community Safety Committee** (subset of Advisory Board):
- Chair: Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)
- Members: Community Safety Lead, Clinical Director, DPO, User Representative
- Mandate: Monthly review of moderation metrics, crisis incidents, enforcement actions, and member safety

#### 4. Legislative Monitoring & Policy Updates

- **Track changes in**:
  - GDPR / UK Data Protection Bill
  - Safeguarding legislation (Care Act 2014, Mental Capacity Act 2005)
  - Consumer Rights Act
  - Equality Act 2010 (accessibility, reasonable adjustments)
  - Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) health claims guidelines
- **Recommend policy updates**: Propose amendments to Board within 30 days of legislative change
- **Ensure ongoing compliance**: Annual compliance audit submitted to Board

#### 5. Quarterly Reporting to Board

Advisory Board submits comprehensive quarterly reports covering:

- **Performance metrics**:
  - Net Promoter Score (NPS)
  - Session completion rates
  - User satisfaction surveys
  - Complaints received and resolved
- **Community safety metrics**:
  - Crisis response times (target: <10 minutes, 95th percentile)
  - Crisis incident summary (suicidal ideation, self-harm, severe distress - anonymized)
  - Safety record (member incidents, near-misses, improvements)
  - Moderation enforcement (posts removed, warnings, bans)
  - Community engagement (active members, growth trends)
- **Compliance status**:
  - Policy review schedule adherence
  - Training completion rates (staff, practitioners)
  - Incident log summary (safeguarding, data breaches, quality concerns)
- **Risk register updates**:
  - New risks identified
  - Mitigation effectiveness
  - Residual risk levels
- **Improvement recommendations**:
  - New content opportunities
  - Technology enhancements
  - Process optimisations
  - Partnership opportunities

## Governance Flow: How It Works in Practice

### Example 1: New Therapeutic Content Release

1. **Content created** by qualified hypnotherapist
2. **Advisory Board reviews** for clinical accuracy, safety, contraindications
3. **User testing** (beta group, feedback collected)
4. **Advisory Board approves** for release
5. **Board notified** via monthly summary report
6. **Post-release monitoring**: User ratings, completion rates, feedback tracked by Advisory Board

### Example 2: Legislative Change (e.g., UK Data Protection Bill passes)

1. **Advisory Board identifies** change via legislative monitoring
2. **DPO assesses impact** on current policies (Privacy Policy, Data Protection Policy, etc.)
3. **Advisory Board recommends** policy amendments to Board
4. **Board approves** updated policies within 30 days
5. **Policies deployed**, users notified via email/in-app
6. **Advisory Board monitors** compliance over next quarter

### Example 3: Safeguarding Incident

1. **Moderator detects** potential safeguarding concern (e.g., abuse disclosure in community)
2. **Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)** immediately notified
3. **DSL assesses risk**, initiates Safeguarding Adults Policy procedures
4. **Advisory Board convenes** emergency meeting within 24 hours (if high-severity)
5. **Board notified** within 4 hours if Priority 0 incident (potential harm, regulatory investigation)
6. **Post-incident review**: Advisory Board investigates root cause, recommends improvements
7. **Board reviews** incident report, approves recommendations, monitors implementation

### Example 4: User Complaint Escalation

1. **User submits complaint** via support@clearminds.com
2. **Customer support** logs complaint, investigates (20-day resolution target per Complaints Policy)
3. **If clinical quality concern**: Escalated to Advisory Board Clinical Director
4. **Advisory Board investigates**: Reviews content, consults practitioner, assesses validity
5. **Recommendations**: Content update, practitioner training, policy clarification
6. **Board informed** via quarterly complaints summary report
7. **User notified** of outcome and improvements made

## Continuous Improvement Culture

### How Governance Drives Quality

1. **Quarterly Risk Assessment Reviews**: Advisory Board updates 8 key risks with new data, adjusts mitigations
2. **Annual External Audits**: Independent audit of data protection, security, moderation effectiveness
3. **User Feedback Loops**: NPS surveys, session ratings, support tickets → Advisory Board analysis → Board action
4. **Industry Benchmarking**: Compare performance vs. competitors (Calm, Headspace, traditional EAPs)
5. **Innovation Pipeline**: Advisory Board recommends new features, Board approves investment, roadmap updated

### Transparency & Accountability

- **Board minutes**: Available to B2B clients on request (commercially sensitive information redacted)
- **Quarterly reports**: Summary performance metrics shared with enterprise partners
- **Annual compliance certification**: Published once achieved (Cyber Essentials Plus, ISO 27001)
- **Incident transparency**: Reportable incidents disclosed to affected clients within 72 hours (GDPR requirement)

## Future Evolution

As ClearMinds scales, the governance structure will evolve:

### Year 1 (Current)
- Board: 3-4 members (founders + 1 NED)
- Advisory Board: 6-7 members (clinical, DPO, legal, industry, user rep)

### Year 2 (5+ Enterprise Clients)
- Board: 4-5 members (add independent NED with EAP/insurance background)
- Advisory Board: 8-10 members (add sector-specific advisors: financial services, healthcare, public sector)

### Year 3 (Scale Phase, £60M target)
- Board: 5-7 members (add Chair, strengthen NEDs)
- Advisory Board: Sub-committees formed:
  - **Clinical Quality Committee**
  - **Data & Compliance Committee**
  - **B2B Client Advisory Committee** (client representatives)
- **External Certifications**: ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, potential CQC registration if service model evolves

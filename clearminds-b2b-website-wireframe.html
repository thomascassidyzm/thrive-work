Our Story: From Data to Delivery

Four years of learning, one strategic pivot, and a commitment to relationships over transactions

Why We Started with B2C (2020-2024)

When we launched ClearMinds four years ago, we could have gone straight to the corporate market. We had the content, the credentials, the connections. But we chose a different path‚Äîand it's made all the difference.

We started with B2C for one simple reason: whether it's B2C or B2B, it ultimately goes to C. It's still an individual mind. Before we could serve corporations effectively, we needed to deeply understand human behavior around wellbeing.

How do people actually engage with hypnotherapy? What makes someone move from curiosity to commitment? When does impulse become habit? These weren't questions we could answer through market research or focus groups. We needed real users, real data, real behavior.

The Learning Phase: What We Built

Those four years became our proving ground. We modeled behavioral patterns‚Äîimpulse purchases versus subscriptions, casual users versus committed members, individual sessions versus comprehensive programs. We made our platform as flexible as possible, learning what worked and what didn't from real choices.

  • 100,000+ users engaged with the platform
  • 520,000 total people have used ClearMinds
  • 3.2M+ website visitors exploring the platform
  • 1.26M app downloads across iOS/Android
  • 34M+ app opens demonstrating sustained engagement
  • 280-day average lifetime after first month (extraordinarily high retention)
  • 18,500 five-star reviews across platforms
  • 35,000+ monthly active users at scale

More recently, we introduced data analysts and specialists who could draw meaningful insights from hundreds of thousands of data points: purchases, utilization patterns, efficacy indicators, engagement trajectories. We tracked where the effort led, what created lasting change, which content resonated most deeply.

By 2024, we had something invaluable: empirical evidence of human behavior around digital hypnotherapy. Not theories. Not projections. Actual data from 100,000+ users showing us exactly how people engage, what they need, and what creates transformation.

The Critical Discovery: Community Amplifies Everything

But the most important learning was this: the best model isn't transactional‚Äîit's relational.

We moved from impulse purchases to subscriptions, from casual users to committed community members, from transactions to trust. We created "super fans" from casual voyeurs through consistent value and genuine support.

By 2024, we had 91,000+ community members with 24/7 professional moderation, crisis response protocols, and a proven safety record. Community wasn't a "nice to have"‚Äîit became the architecture that makes digital mental health intervention actually work.

That's when we knew we were ready for B2B. Not because we thought we knew it all, but because we had facts and evidence to back every claim.

The Strategic Pivot (2024-2025)

Having harvested data and proven our approach with real users, we made a deliberate pivot. We onboarded expertise from the corporate world‚Äîspecifically from highly regulated markets like healthcare, insurance, and financial services. People who understand compliance, governance, and the careful dance of B2B relationships in sensitive sectors.

Why We Brought On Corporate Expertise

We recognized something critical from our B2C experience: people don't just wake up and demand wellbeing services. They need to be guided. Most people think they know what meditation is, or hypnotherapy, but there's tremendous value in proper education. Understanding the science, the mechanism, the "why it works" makes all the difference in engagement and results.

So we brought on specialists in AI, education backgrounds, and regulatory frameworks. Not because we love complexity, but because effective wellbeing requires effective education.

Why Health, Wellbeing & Insurance?

We could have targeted any sector. Tech, finance, retail‚Äîthey all need workplace wellbeing. But we focused on health, wellbeing, and insurance for a specific reason drawn from our own lived experience: it doesn't seem well catered for or easy to access in these sectors.

Think about it: insurance companies sell wellbeing benefits, healthcare organizations employ wellbeing professionals, yet their own employees often struggle to access effective support. The irony isn't lost on us.

We saw an opportunity to serve the people who serve others. And we had exactly what they needed: something immediately available, on-demand, that fits anyone's capacity to pick it up and put it down. For a busy workforce juggling client needs, regulatory demands, and high-stress environments? That flexibility is essential.

From Transactional to Relational

Here's what four years of B2C taught us: the best model isn't transactional‚Äîit's relational.

B2C can be faddish. People try things on impulse, influenced by inflation, cost of living, the latest wellness trend. They pick things up, put them down, try multiple apps. There's value there, but there's also instability.

We'd already started moving away from that in our B2C model‚Äîbuilding subscriptions, fostering community, creating super fans from casual voyeurs. That shift was the forebearer of our B2B direction. It proved we could build trust, create communities, and turn casual interest into committed transformation.

B2B Amplifies Everything We Learned to Do Well

It's inherently relational, not transactional. It's built on trust and long-term relationships. It allows us to use our customer service expertise in a concentrated, meaningful way rather than spreading it thin across thousands of individual impulse buyers.

So we partnered with people who have 20-30 years of experience in specific sectors‚Äîrecruitment, healthcare, insurance. People who understand that B2B is relational, who've built careers on trust and partnership, who know how to listen to what organizations actually need.

The Investment Shift

Here's what's changed in our resource allocation: instead of channeling money into broad B2C marketing (chasing impulse buyers alongside subscriptions), we're pivoting investment into what B2B actually needs:

  • Enhanced customer service and onboarding (Rachel Goodley and her team)
  • Data analytics and reporting (showing what's working, what's not)
  • Partnership management (deeper relationships, better listening)
  • B2B-specific content development (workplace-focused modules)
  • Educational resources (helping organizations understand hypnotherapy)

We're reducing spray-and-pray marketing spend and concentrating resources on high-yielding relationships where we can make real impact.

Our B2B Promise

Our aim now is clear: assist our B2B partners to amplify their brand trustworthiness and expertise with our unique offer to their customers.

When we partner with an organization like Towergate, we're not just a vendor in their benefits portfolio. We're invested in their success. We bring them a unique, brilliant, proven, trusted brand‚Äîand then we partner with them to shape it for their specific workforce needs.

We have more time within customer relationships to listen, to understand downstream needs, to refine and adapt. Our team with extensive corporate reach is already talking to businesses across sectors‚Äîrecruitment firms dealing with productivity loss and isolation, healthcare organizations managing burnout, insurance companies addressing fear and anxiety among client-facing staff.

We Listen. We Adapt. We Deliver.

We act transparently, work closely with client-based organizations, and prioritize their success alongside ours. Because we learned in B2C that the path from casual interest to committed transformation is built on trust and value.

That's the ClearMinds journey. Four years of learning how human beings engage with wellbeing. One strategic pivot to serve organizations who serve others. And a commitment to relationships over transactions, trust over marketing, and lasting transformation over quick fixes.

This is who we are. This is why we're ready. This is what we offer.

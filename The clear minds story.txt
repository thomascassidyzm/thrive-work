# The ClearMinds Journey: From Data to Delivery


## Why We Started with B2C (2020-2024)


When we launched ClearMinds four years ago, we could have gone straight to the corporate market. We had the content, the credentials, the connections. But we chose a different path—and it's made all the difference.


We started with B2C for one simple reason: **whether it's B2C or B2B, it ultimately goes to C. It's still an individual mind.** Before we could serve corporations effectively, we needed to deeply understand human behavior around wellbeing. How do people actually engage with hypnotherapy? What makes someone move from curiosity to commitment? When does impulse become habit?


Those four years became our proving ground. We modelled behavioral patterns—impulse purchases versus subscriptions, casual users versus committed members, individual sessions versus comprehensive programs. We made our platform as flexible as possible, learning what worked and what didn't from real users making real choices.


More recently, we introduced data analysts and specialists who could draw meaningful insights from hundreds of thousands of data points: purchases, utilization patterns, efficacy indicators, engagement trajectories. We tracked where the effort led, what created lasting change, which content resonated most deeply.


By 2024, we had something invaluable: **empirical evidence of human behavior around digital hypnotherapy.** Not theories. Not projections. Actual data from 100,000+ users showing us exactly how people engage, what they need, and what creates transformation.


We also built something else crucial: a robust library of proven therapies, refined through four years of user feedback. Content that works because thousands of people showed us what works.


**That's when we knew we were ready for B2B.** Not because we thought we knew it all, but because we had facts and evidence to back every claim.


## The Strategic Pivot (2024-2025)


Having harvested data and proven our approach with real users, we made a deliberate pivot. We onboarded expertise from the corporate world—specifically from highly regulated markets like healthcare, insurance, and financial services. People who understand compliance, governance, and the careful dance of B2B relationships in sensitive sectors.


We also recognized something critical from our B2C experience: **people don't just wake up and demand wellbeing services. They need to be guided.** Most people think they know what meditation is, or hypnotherapy, but there's tremendous value in proper education. Understanding the science, the mechanism, the "why it works" makes all the difference in engagement and results.


So we brought on specialists in AI, education backgrounds, and regulatory frameworks. Not because we love complexity, but because **effective wellbeing requires effective education.**


## Why Health, Wellbeing & Insurance?


We could have targeted any sector. Tech, finance, retail—they all need workplace wellbeing. But we focused on **health, wellbeing, and insurance** for a specific reason drawn from our own lived experience: it doesn't seem well catered for or easy to access in these sectors.


Think about it: insurance companies sell wellbeing benefits, healthcare organizations employ wellbeing professionals, yet their own employees often struggle to access effective support. The irony isn't lost on us.


We saw an opportunity to serve the people who serve others. And we had exactly what they needed: **something immediately available, on-demand, that fits anyone's capacity to pick it up and put it down.** For a busy workforce juggling client needs, regulatory demands, and high-stress environments? That flexibility is essential.


## From Transactional to Relational


Here's what four years of B2C taught us: **the best model isn't transactional—it's relational.**


B2C can be faddish. People try things on impulse, influenced by inflation, cost of living, the latest wellness trend. They pick things up, put them down, try multiple apps. There's value there, but there's also instability.


We'd already started moving away from that in our B2C model—building subscriptions, fostering community, creating super fans from casual voyeurs. That shift was the forebearer of our B2B direction. It proved we could build trust, create communities, and turn casual interest into committed transformation.


**B2B amplifies everything we learned to do well.** It's inherently relational, not transactional. It's built on trust and long-term relationships. It allows us to use our customer service expertise in a concentrated, meaningful way rather than spreading it thin across thousands of individual impulse buyers.


So we partnered with people who have 20-30 years of experience in specific sectors—recruitment, healthcare, insurance. People who understand that **B2B is relational**, who've built careers on trust and partnership, who know how to listen to what organizations actually need.


## Purpose-Built for the Workforce


Along this journey, we've learned to focus relentlessly on what matters: **customer service couched in human connection and clear communication.** Not automated responses, not chatbots with canned answers, but real people providing real support.


We've also developed content specifically for workplace challenges. Not just adapting our consumer content, but creating entirely new modules: Decompress at Work, Stress and Anxiety in Professional Settings, Better Communication, Active Listening, Overcoming Isolation, Managing Fear in High-Pressure Roles.


These emerged from conversations with our B2B partners. Recruiters told us about the isolation and anxiety of remote workers. Insurance professionals described the stress of client-facing roles during difficult claims. Healthcare workers shared the emotional toll of caregiving.


**We listened. Then we created.**


## The Investment Shift


Here's what's changed in our resource allocation: instead of channeling money into broad B2C marketing (chasing impulse buyers alongside subscriptions), **we're pivoting investment into what B2B actually needs:**


- **Enhanced customer service and onboarding** (Rachel and her team)
- **Data analytics and reporting** (showing what's working, what's not)
- **Partnership management** (deeper relationships, better listening)
- **B2B-specific content development** (workplace-focused modules)
- **Educational resources** (helping organizations understand hypnotherapy)


We're reducing spray-and-pray marketing spend and concentrating resources on high-yielding relationships where we can make real impact.


## Our B2B Promise


Our aim now is clear: **assist our B2B partners to amplify their brand trustworthiness and expertise with our unique offer to their customers.**


When we partner with an organization like Towergate, we're not just a vendor in their benefits portfolio. We're invested in their success. We bring them a unique, brilliant, proven, trusted brand—and then we partner with them to shape it for their specific workforce needs.


We have more time within customer relationships to listen, to understand downstream needs, to refine and adapt. Our team with extensive corporate reach is already talking to businesses across sectors—recruitment firms dealing with productivity loss and isolation, healthcare organizations managing burnout, insurance companies addressing fear and anxiety among client-facing staff.


**We listen. We adapt. We deliver.**


We act transparently, work closely with client-based organizations, and prioritize their success alongside ours. Because we learned in B2C that the path from casual interest to committed transformation is built on trust and value.


Now we're bringing that same approach—refined, tested, proven—to the organizations who need it most.


That's the ClearMinds journey. Four years of learning how human beings engage with wellbeing. One strategic pivot to serve organizations who serve others. And a commitment to relationships over transactions, trust over marketing, and lasting transformation over quick fixes.


**This is who we are. This is why we're ready. This is what we offer.**

Executive Summary
The UK faces a £51-65 billion annual crisis in workplace mental health. Current solutions are demonstrably failing: traditional EAPs achieve only 3-5% utilization, NHS Talking Therapies see 60% dropout rates, and meditation apps require indefinite daily practice with no resolution endpoint.
ClearMinds spent four years deliberately building what the market needs: a mental health resolution platform that combines 65 years of clinical hypnotherapy research with proven community engagement, delivering lasting pattern change in 6-18 hours rather than indefinite symptom management.
With 100,000+ users, 91,000+ community members, and empirical data proving 6x better engagement than traditional EAPs, we're ready to launch the category we've been building. We need the right partner to do it with.
Towergate's CEO challenged us: "Don't be another EAP. Create something distinctive."
This memo outlines how we do exactly that—and what Towergate becomes as first mover in a £51 billion market.

Part 1: The Founding Vision and Market Context
Why ClearMinds Exists
The founding vision of ClearMinds was, and remains to this day, a community-first approach. The company was launched in 2021, amidst the pandemic and in the immediate aftermath, as most of us found ourselves trying to find and fit with a 'new normalcy.' It presented a seismic shift in how we work and live.
For some, this involved a whole new work-life rebalance. For others it entailed new work routines including working from home. For almost everyone affected, these changes came with their own challenges—ones which brought to the conscious mind how we live and work and the need to address things which appeared to be holding us back or disrupted our balance.
The Compound Crisis That Followed
The explosion of wearable tech which then followed would constantly tell us if we were not sleeping or if we were anxious but would not venture a solution or help. What followed was increasing demand for psychological support and medical intervention, all of which also came with challenges from cost to availability, as well as the fundamental issue of 'where to start.'
By 2023/24, with the emerging patterns and challenges becoming ever clearer, we knew it was time to take the established product and the power of community direct into the workforce.
As we opened discussions with experienced professionals, supported by our own experts with direct experience in highly regulated sectors, we could also see the compounding effect of AI and how this was causing even greater degrees of uncertainty and anxiety around job retention and progression. All of which was adding to the pressure on mental health, bleeding across work, life and family communities.
The Communication Paradox
Paradoxically, at a time where communication between executives, managers and the wider workforce was of paramount importance, evolving legislation around disabilities and rights to reasonable adjustments stopped many employers from opening discussions with employees when it became reasonably clear that individuals were struggling. All of which only served to widen the gap of trust-based communication which affects the underlying culture of any organisation.
This is the context in which we're operating: A compound crisis of post-pandemic adjustment, wearable tech anxiety (data without solutions), AI-driven job uncertainty, and legislative complexity that has eroded the very communication channels needed to address mental health effectively.
Organizations need solutions that work within this reality—not generic wellness programs designed for simpler times.

Part 4: The Market Failure Creating This Opportunity
The £51 Billion Crisis
UK employers lose £51-65 billion annually to poor mental health. This isn't a projection—it's documented reality:
   * £24 billion in presenteeism (largest component)
   * £22 billion in staff turnover costs
   * £6 billion in absenteeism
   * 17.1 million working days lost to mental health annually
   * 1 in 6 workers experiencing mental health problems
   * 63% showing burnout symptoms (up from 51% pre-COVID)
Post-COVID, employers saw a 60% increase in mental health-related absences. Mental health is now the #1 employer concern, yet the solutions available are demonstrably inadequate.
Why Current Solutions Are Failing
NHS Talking Therapies:
   * 47-day median wait time
   * 60% dropout rate (only 37% complete treatment)
   * 49.9% recovery rate (below the 50% target)
   * 78% forced to use emergency services while waiting
   * Regional variation: recovery rates from 32-57%
Traditional EAP:
   * 3-5% utilization despite 24.45 million UK employees covered
   * Generic counseling with no condition-specific protocols
   * 60% signposted to self-help, only 20% get therapy
   * No measurable outcomes or resolution endpoint
   * 44% of organizations considering changing provider
   * 16% planning to cancel entirely
Meditation Apps (Headspace, Calm):
   * Prevention/wellness, not clinical intervention
   * Requires ongoing daily practice indefinitely
   * Stop practicing → symptoms return
   * Cannot address clinical conditions requiring resolution
   * Moderate adoption but no clinical pathways
The Gap: Management vs. Resolution
Here's the fundamental issue: employers are paying for ongoing management of conditions that could be resolved in 6-18 hours of clinical intervention.
Traditional approaches manage symptoms. They provide coping strategies. They offer support during crisis. But they don't resolve the underlying patterns causing the problem.
That's not a criticism—it's a category limitation. EAPs weren't designed to resolve conditions. They were designed to provide access to support. Meditation apps weren't designed to treat clinical conditions. They were designed for stress management and mindfulness.
The market needs something different: a resolution platform.

Part 5: The Clinical Foundation for Resolution
65 Years of Research
Clinical hypnotherapy isn't experimental. It's one of the most extensively researched mental health interventions available:
   * 65+ years of peer-reviewed research
   * AMA recognition since 1958
   * Systematic reviews and meta-analyses across multiple conditions
   * NICE recognition for specific conditions (IBS)
The Mechanism: Why Resolution Is Possible
The key difference between hypnotherapy and other interventions lies in the mechanism:
Meditation/Mindfulness:
   * Builds conscious self-regulatory capacity
   * Strengthens prefrontal control over automatic responses
   * Requires ongoing daily practice to maintain benefits
   * Manages symptoms while practicing
   * Stop practicing → symptoms return
Clinical Hypnotherapy:
   * Bypasses critical faculty to access subconscious patterns
   * Directly reprograms neural pathways causing symptoms
   * Achieves lasting pattern change in 6-18 hours of intervention

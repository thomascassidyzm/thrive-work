How This Can Work to Better Serve You

UK Legal Framework - Equality Act 2010

In the UK, employees can insist on reasonable adjustments for conditions not formally diagnosed. The Equality Act 2010 focuses on the effects of impairment, not formal diagnosis—employers must focus on the effect on employee's work.

Employers "ought reasonably to know" about disabilities if they receive enough information about potential issues.

  • No diagnosis required for adjustments
  • Focus on effects, not labels
  • Employer duty to act on knowledge

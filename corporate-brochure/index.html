UK Legal Framework - Equality Act 2010

In the UK, an employee can insist on reasonable adjustments for a condition that has not been formally diagnosed. The legal duty under the Equality Act 2010 focuses on the effects of a physical or mental impairment, not on a formal diagnosisâ€”which means that the employer should not use the lack of a formal diagnosis as a reason to do nothing. They must focus on the effect of the impairment on the employee's work.

In the context of UK reasonable adjustments law, an employer "ought reasonably to know" about an employee's disability if they have received enough information to be put on notice of a potential issue, even if they were not explicitly told.

  • No formal diagnosis required for reasonable adjustments
  • Focus on effects, not diagnosis labels
  • Employer duty to act on reasonable knowledge
